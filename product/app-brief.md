## Product One-Liner

w(inner) helps elite and aspiring athletes generate personalized visualization audio for their sport, position, and current mental performance challenge.

## Positioning

- w(inner) is not a meditation app.
- w(inner) is not a chatbot.
- w(inner) is not framed as therapy.
- w(inner) is a structured psychological training system for athletes.
- The product should feel closer to a private performance coach than a wellness app.
- The brand should feel premium, restrained, intentional, calm, and high-end.

## Value Proposition

Primary line:

> Play like you know you can.

Expanded version:

Athletes often have the physical ability but fail to access it under pressure. w(inner) gives them a personal mental training system built around their sport, position, opponents, venues, triggers, and performance struggles.

## Product Pillars

| Pillar | Purpose | Typical length | Voice role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Daily Visualization | Morning identity programming and long-term mental baseline work. | Around 5 minutes | Mindset Coach |
| Scenario-Based | Mental reps, technique drilling, error correction, and training-week situations. | 3-5 minutes | Technical Coach |
| Match Day Prep | Pre-competition state management, night-before anchor, and game-day activation. | 3-10 minutes | Performance Manager |

## Core Product Flow

1. Athlete builds a personal Athlete Identity.
2. Onboarding captures name, gender, sport, position, and honest struggles.
3. The app computes a starting mental baseline across Confidence, Focus, Vision, Resilience, Composure, and Drive.
4. The athlete chooses a pillar based on what they need today.
5. The app runs a structured AI diagnostic chat with four questions.
6. The app generates a custom spoken visualization using xAI TTS.
7. The athlete sees projected attribute impact before listening.
8. Optional reflection updates attributes only when the athlete reports real-world experience.

## Functional Claims

These are factual product capabilities from the brief.

- The app generates personalized visualization audio tracks on demand.
- The app uses an AI diagnostic chat to collect situation-specific details.
- The app creates spoken narration the athlete can listen to before training, before sleep, or before competition.
- The system has three pillars and nine tracks.
- The app tracks six mental attributes: Confidence, Focus, Vision, Resilience, Composure, and Drive.
- The app shows a radar chart based on honest self-assessment.
- The app can personalize by sport, position, opponent, venue, trigger, and scenario.
- Optional check-ins can update the athlete's attributes based on reported real-world experience.

## Benefit Angles

Use these as approved direction, but still flag strong outcome claims in `claim-bank.md`.

### Performance

- Talent-performance gap: the athlete has skill but cannot access it when it matters.
- Game-day confidence: show up certain instead of nervous.
- Clutch performance: want the moment instead of hiding from it.
- Mental reps: train technique, tactics, and presence when the body cannot train.
- Slump breaker: interrupt the missed-shot, bad-game, or error loop before it becomes identity.

### Emotional

- Self-doubt before big moments.
- Pre-competition anxiety.
- Recovery from failure.
- Imposter syndrome at a higher level.
- Fear of letting down parents, coaches, teammates, scouts, or self.

### Identity

- Becoming the version of yourself that wins.
- Modeling a hero's traits and presence.
- Linking daily training to scholarship, championship, contract, or long-term goal.
- Leadership identity for captains and vocal players.

### Competitive Edge

- The remaining 1 percent after physical work.
- Pre-trial, pre-scout, and one-shot preparation.
- Returning from injury.
- Transitioning to a new position, system, coach, or level.

### Habit

- Morning operating system.
- Night-before anchor.
- Walkout ritual.
- Travel-friendly mental gym.

## Sports And Example Struggles

The app is sport-agnostic. Sport-specific language should come from onboarding, position, struggle pills, and diagnostic answers.

| Sport | Example struggles | Product angle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soccer | 1v1 confidence, first-touch overthinking, penalty fear | Identity work, first-touch scenario reps, kickoff activation |
| Basketball | Free-throw nerves, open-shot hesitation, clutch freezing | Missed-shot rewrite, night-before anchor, hero modeling |
| Football | Pre-snap anxiety, missed reads, post-fumble spiral | Read repetition, worst-case prep, confidence identity |
| Tennis | Double-fault spirals, break-point choking, focus between points | Serve correction, purpose anchoring, tournament anchor |
| Golf | Yips, swing-thought overload, first-tee nerves | Swing-feel blueprint, tournament identity, night-before round anchor |
| Combat sports | Pre-fight dread, freezing after first hit, fading late | Worst-case rehearsal, walkout activation, fighter identity |
| Endurance | False-start anxiety, final-stretch fade, racing scared | Start sequence, stride/stroke visualization, long-term drive |
| Team sports | Service errors, bad shifts, communication breakdowns | Error rewrite, leadership identity, tournament prep |
| Bat-and-ball sports | Slumps, mechanics overthinking, long-format focus | Technique review, slump rewrite, pre-game anchor |
| Esports | Tilt, queue anxiety, focus collapse | Tilt rewrite, tournament identity, match prep |
