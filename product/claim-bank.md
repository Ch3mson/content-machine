# w(inner) Claim Bank

This file keeps app claims from drifting. Marketing-first ideas are allowed, but unverified claims must stay flagged until approved.

## Claim Policy

- Use approved claims freely in account playbooks and sample slides.
- Use flagged claims only for ideation or drafts marked for review.
- Do not move a flagged claim into a final source-of-truth file until it is approved.
- Do not make medical, therapeutic, guaranteed performance, scholarship, contract, or victory claims.
- If a claim depends on data, testimonials, product screenshots, expert support, or research, link the proof.

## Bold Marketing Hypotheses

These may be useful angles, but they need approval before becoming final rules.

| Hypothesis | Why it could work | Risk | Needed proof or edit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| "Play like you know you can." | Brief-approved tagline with strong emotional fit. | Could imply guaranteed performance if paired with hard claims. | Approved as tagline; avoid guarantee context. |
| "The 5-minute morning routine that turns athletes into closers." | Strong hook for Daily Visualization. | "Turns" overpromises transformation. | Consider "helps athletes rehearse closing moments." |
| "Train the version of you that shows up." | Fits identity positioning. | Abstract if unsupported by product example. | Pair with specific track or scenario. |
| "Mental reps when your body cannot train." | Strong for injury, recovery, travel, and off-days. | Recovery claims may become medical. | Keep to visualization and confidence, not healing. |
| "The 1 percent edge." | Fits elite athlete angle. | Common marketing phrase; may feel generic. | Use only when the competitor flow supports competitive-edge framing. |

## Flagged For Approval

| Draft claim | Where it may appear | Why it is flagged | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| "Build unshakeable game-day confidence." | Hooks, captions, final slide | Strong outcome claim. | Pending approval/proof. |
| "Silence the voice that holds you back." | Hooks, body slides | Could sound therapeutic if framed poorly. | Use as emotional language, not mental health claim. |
| "Rewire how you show up." | Product explanation | Strong psychological claim. | Needs careful wording or support. |
| "Become the athlete you were meant to be." | Identity angle | Aspirational but broad. | Use sparingly. |
| "Track mental performance over time." | Product slide or caption | Needs clarity that attributes are self-reported, not clinical measurements. | Use with self-assessment context. |
| "Sleep the night before competition." | Match Day/Night-before angle | Sleep outcome claim. | Reframe as "night-before anchor" unless supported. |

## Rejected Or High-Risk Claims

| Claim | Reason rejected | Replacement wording |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Cures anxiety." | Medical/therapeutic claim. | "Helps athletes prepare for pressure moments." |
| "Guarantees clutch performance." | Guaranteed outcome. | "Built for the moments where athletes usually hesitate." |
| "Get a scholarship with mental training." | Outcome guarantee. | "Prepare for high-stakes trials and scout games." |
| "Fixes your confidence." | Overclaim. | "Gives athletes a structured confidence routine." |
| "Clinically proven mental performance." | No proof in brief. | Do not use unless proof is added. |
