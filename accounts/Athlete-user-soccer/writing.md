## Core Voice Snapshot

- Audience: Competitive youth/club soccer players (14-22), aspiring to play at higher levels
- Voice: Blunt and experienced. Says the uncomfortable truth without being cruel. Aggressive tones used aspirationally, not just to tear down. Speaks like a player who's been through the levels, not a coach lecturing from a manual.
- Promise: Actionable advice that makes you better — not inspiration that makes you feel better.
- Emotional lever: Competitive pressure + hidden knowledge. The reader feels like they're being let in on what the best players actually do.
- Authority style: Earned through personal observation ("I've seen...") and specific behaviors. Never borrowed from titles or institutions.
- Rhythm: Short declarative sentences. One idea per slide. Default flow is hook + list modules, not a mini-essay arc.

## Locked Rules

1. **Never use generic motivation.** If it could appear on a locker room poster or in a Nike ad, rewrite it. The user explicitly banned this.
2. **Start with a specific reader frustration or desire.** The hook must feel personal, not universal.
3. **Default to list-first flows.** A normal post should be a hook followed by 3-5 numbered/listed items, rules, mistakes, contrasts, or habits. Do not write a slow lesson arc unless the user explicitly asks for one.
4. **One idea per slide.** If a slide has two concepts, split it into two slides.
5. **Name the behavior.** Replace every adverb with a specific action. "Train harder" → "Add 2 extra reps when you think you're done."
6. **End on the final list item, not a moral.** Most reference user posts do not need a separate conclusion slide. The last listed item should land the post.
7. **Use negative framing for positive payloads.** "3 things to avoid if you wanna go pro" beats "3 things to do."

## Draft Or Candidate Rules

1. **Sell identity, not information.** Candidate rule — needs quality-gate validation. Identity hooks (tgaskins3 posts 4-5) outperformed tactical hooks (tgaskins3 posts 2-3) in reference classification.
2. **Vulnerability is your credential.** Candidate rule — needs quality-gate validation. kingdubar post 1 suggests failure-confession is a strong trust trigger.
3. **Contrast is compression.** Candidate rule — kingdubar post 2 proves 2 slides can replace 6 with a strong contrast.
