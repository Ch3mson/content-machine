# Flow Demo: 3 Things That Tell Your Coach You're Not Ready (with w(inner))

**Hook archetype**: `avoid-list` → `identity-transformation`
**Slide count**: 6
**Status**: Corrected demo — list-first, with native w(inner) product integration

---

## Slide 1 — Hook

> 5 things that tell your coach you're not ready to start.
> And none of them are about talent.

---

## Slide 2 — Item 1

> 1. You stop when the drill gets hard.
> Coaches notice who fades in the last rep.
> The player who finishes every drill like it's the first one — that's who gets the minutes.

---

## Slide 3 — Item 2

> 2. You only play when you have the ball.
> 80 minutes without it.
> If your movement off the ball is walking, your coach sees it.
> The game is won in the spaces between touches.

---

## Slide 4 — Item 3

> 3. You make the same mistake every week.
> Turning over the same ball. Losing the same runner.
> Coaches can forgive a mistake once.
> Twice means you're not learning.

---

## Slide 5 — Item 4

> 4. You let one bad moment turn into three.
> Miss a pass. Drop your head. Miss another.
> Your body language tells your coach you're gone before the whistle.
> That's what gets you subbed off.

---

## Slide 6 — Item 5 (w(inner))

> 5. Most players don't know how to reset between moments.
> I use w(inner) for that.
> It builds the mental routine that keeps my head in the game.
> Your coach can't see what's inside your head.
> But they can see the result.
