## Candidate Samples

### Candidate 1: Identity-Transformation (from tgaskins3 post 4 adaptation)

- Source: Adapted from tgaskins3 "How to be a RUTHLESS KILLER on the pitch"
- What works: Identity framing, short rules, every slide has one specific behavior. List-first identity ladder.
- What needs revision: TBD — needs user calibration

```text
How to be the player coaches trust in the 85th minute.

1. Know your job before the ball arrives.
Most players react. The trusted one already decided.

2. Make the simple pass first.
Fancy assists get replays. Simple passes win games.
Coaches notice who keeps possession.

3. Talk when you're tired.
Silence on the pitch is a signal.
The player who's still communicating in the 80th is the one they keep on.

4. Win your 1v1 before the pass comes.
If you need to look, you've already lost it.

5. Be in the right spot every time.
Not flashy. Not lucky. Reliable.
```

### Candidate 2: Avoid-List (from laviedem30 adaptation)

- Source: Adapted from laviedem30 "3 Things to avoid if you wanna go pro"
- What works: Negative framing makes positive content feel like secret knowledge
- What needs revision: TBD — needs user calibration

```text
3 things to avoid if you wanna go pro.

1. Training the same way every session.
Your body adapts. If you always do the same drills, you stop improving.
Pro players periodize. They know when to push and when to recover.

2. Eating like it doesn't matter.
You can out-train a bad diet for about 2 seasons.
Then your body starts breaking down.
The guys who make it treat food as fuel, not a reward.

3. Only doing what your coach tells you.
Your coach has 20 players to worry about.
You have one.
Elite players do extra. Not because they're told. Because they know what they need.
```

### Candidate 3: Invisible-Competition (from tgaskins3 post 1 adaptation)

- Source: Adapted from tgaskins3 "While you skipped your last rep..."
- What works: Pressure without lecturing. Each slide is a complete contrast. The repeated "while you" structure functions as an implied list.
- What needs revision: TBD — needs user calibration

```text
4 things someone else is doing while you make excuses.

1. While you're sleeping in, someone else is doing extra conditioning.
Just so you know.

2. While you're skipping leg day, someone else is building the explosive power you wish you had.
Just so you know.

3. While you're making excuses about your touch, someone else is doing 200 reps against a wall.
Just so you know.

4. While you're blaming your coach for not playing you, someone else is making themselves undroppable.
Just so you know.
```
