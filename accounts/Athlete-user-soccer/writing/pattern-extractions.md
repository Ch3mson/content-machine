## Viewer-Perspective Notes

For each post, ask:

### What makes the viewer stop?

- **Competitive pressure** (tgaskins3 post 1, kingdubar post 2): The viewer stops because the hook names their fear — someone else is working harder, or they're doing the wrong things. It feels personal.
- **Identity promise** (tgaskins3 posts 4-5): The viewer stops because they want to be that thing. "RUTHLESS KILLER" or "EGO" — charged words that promise a stronger identity.
- **Vulnerability + authority** (kingdubar post 1): The viewer stops because someone who failed is now telling them how to win. The failure makes the advice credible.
- **Hidden knowledge** (laviedem30 posts 2-4, marcogetsfit posts 2-4): The viewer stops because it feels like they're being let in on a secret. "Things to avoid" or "unusual habits" — the framing promises information they don't already have.
- **Branded expertise** (aura-fit3 posts 1,3,5): The viewer stops because "elite ball knowledge" sounds exclusive and insider. The casual confidence sells the expertise.

### What private frustration or desire does the post name?

- "I'm not working hard enough" / "Someone is beating me"
- "I want to be seen as tough/dangerous/elite"
- "I'm making mistakes I don't know about"
- "I want the edge nobody else has"
- "I want fast results"

### What promise keeps the viewer swiping?

- Each slide adds one more comparison, rule, habit, or fix. The viewer stays because the next slide either completes the list or escalates the tension.
- In contrast structures (kingdubar post 2, marcogetsfit posts): the viewer stays for the reveal of what the good version looks like.
- In identity structures (tgaskins3 posts 4-5): the viewer stays because each slide defines a rule of the identity they want to embody.

### Where does trust come from?

- **Personal experience** (kingdubar): "I was there, I failed, here's what I learned." Highest trust.
- **Hidden knowledge framing** (laviedem30): Trust comes from the implication that the author knows things most people don't.
- **Identity confidence** (tgaskins3): Trust comes from the author's willingness to say uncomfortable things directly.
- **Consistency** (aura-fit3): Trust from having a repeatable branded voice across posts.

### What feels copied, generic, or weak?

- Avoid-list format without the pro-level insight underneath (just generic bad advice).
- Identity hooks that are all attitude and no actionable content.
- Numbered lists where the numbers are filler, not distinct insights.
- Any hook that sounds like "motivation" instead of "instruction."

## Candidate Patterns

0. **The List Spine** (from nearly every user reference): The native account shape is hook + list modules. Slide 1 sets the promise. Slides 2-4/6 deliver numbered or implied items: mistakes, rules, habits, contrasts, fundamentals, or steps. The viewer keeps swiping because the list is incomplete, not because an essay arc is unfolding.

1. **The Avoid Flip** (from laviedem30, marcogetsfit post 1): "3 things to avoid" wrapper that actually reveals positive pro-level habits. Each slide names a negative behavior then immediately replaces it with the right behavior. The avoid framing makes the advice feel like discovered truth rather than obvious tips.

2. **The Identity Ladder** (from tgaskins3 posts 4-5): Define a charged identity trait, then give 4-7 rules to embody it. Each rule is a slide. The rules are aspirational but behavioral — things you actually do, not things you feel.

3. **The Contrast Punch** (from kingdubar post 2): Two-slide mini-format. Slide 1: what most athletes do. Slide 2: what the 1% do. No explanation needed — the contrast is the argument.

4. **The Invisible Competition** (from tgaskins3 post 1): "While you [normal behavior], someone else [elite behavior]. Just so you know." Each slide is a different comparison. The "just so you know" close keeps it from feeling like a lecture.

5. **The Unusual Timer** (from marcogetsfit posts 3-4): Time-lock habits to a specific period — pre-game, post-training, morning of game day, night before tryouts. The time context makes generic habits feel specific and intentional.

6. **The Branded Voice Frame** (from aura-fit3): Own a branded phrase that carries across posts. "Elite ball knowledge," "Real talk," "What nobody tells you" — a recurring identifier that builds account identity.

## Rejected Patterns

- Pure chronology posts (ashton.schumann post 1 style) — too long for saves/shares goal, too biographical for branded identity approach.
- Pure science protocol (chasingpeaks0 style) — too clinical for the blunt/aspirational tone.
- Pure transformation promise without tactical content (aura-fit3 post 2/4 style) — feels like hype without substance.
- Slow mini-essay arcs — hook → wrong behavior → right behavior → test → moral close. This may be good advice, but it does not match the majority of user-reference account flows, which are list-first.
