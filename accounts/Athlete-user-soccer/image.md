# Image Rules: Athlete User Soccer Account

Use this file for photo direction, visual treatment, and selected-photo
suitability. Use `design.md` for canvas size, layout, typography, text
placement, crop dimensions, and renderer-facing format rules.

Do not use this file as the source of truth for text layout.

## Visual Treatment

- Keep photos in color. This is a native platform account, not a vintage/storytelling account.
- No B&W conversion.
- No vintage paper overlay.
- Keep natural contrast levels — do not crush shadows or blow highlights.
- Ensure text readability by choosing photos with consistent, non-busy regions for text placement (sky, grass, wall, bench, plain background).
- Avoid photos where the background is so busy or high-contrast that white text with a 3px outline becomes unreadable.
- If a photo has a busy background, prefer shots where a uniform region (sky, pitch grass, wall, training bib, net) sits in the center third of the frame.

## Pipeline Order

The processing pipeline must run in this order:

1. Frame the selected photo according to `design.md`.
2. Add text according to `design.md`.
3. No additional filters, overlays, or treatments.

## Photo Crop Notes

- Target canvas and crop dimensions come from `design.md`.
- Crop from center. Never stretch or distort the image.
- Keep the player, face, or key action in the readable center of the frame.
- If a source image is too narrow for the canvas, pad with black bars instead of stretching.

## Photo Selection Principles

- Prefer candid or action photos over posed or press photos. The account should feel like a real player sharing real moments.
- Goal: photos should look like they could have been taken by a teammate, not a professional photographer.
- Acceptable source types: training ground shots, game action, dressing room, pitch-side, travel, warm-up, recovery.
- Avoid: stock photo looks, overly polished press photos, obvious Getty Images cropping.
- For product slides: native phone-in-hand shot or a natural training context. No product shots that look like app store screenshots.

## Product Slide Treatment

- The product slide uses a native phone-in-hand or training-context photo.
- Keep the same color, no-filter treatment as the rest of the post.
- No additional branding or logos on the image.
- Product mention is woven into the copy, not the design.

## Image Role Notes

- Use `design.md` for slide count, layout, typography, text placement, and renderer-facing rules.
- Use `writing.md` and the target `flow.md` for copy rhythm and slide text.
- Use this file only to decide whether a selected photo fits the intended slide role and visual treatment.
