# Account Brief

## Account Basics

- Account name: athlete-user-soccer
- Niche: Soccer — branded user-type account giving blunt, experienced advice that feels native to the platform
- Target audience: Competitive youth/club soccer players (14-22), aspiring to play at higher levels
- Platform priority: TikTok first (9:16 slideshows), Instagram secondary
- Primary performance goal: Saves and shares — value-driven content players bookmark and send to teammates
- Product relationship to w(inner): Subtle/camouflaged — extremely native integration where it fits naturally

## Audience Understanding

- What the audience wants: Real, unfiltered advice from someone who's been through the levels. Not coach-speak, not motivational fluff. They want to know what actually separates good from elite.
- What the audience struggles with: [Open Question — will refine through VOC research]
- What they already believe: [Open Question]
- What they are tired of hearing: Generic motivation quotes, empty platitudes, "just work harder" without specifics, coaching manual jargon
- What would make them save/share: Tactical, mental, and lifestyle advice that feels like inside knowledge — the stuff nobody tells you but everyone needs to hear
- What would make the account feel fake: AI-sounding writing, borrowed authority without personal experience, content that sounds like it was written by someone who never played

## Account Point Of View

- Core belief: Most soccer advice is too soft and too generic. The real difference-makers are mental, hidden, and uncomfortable.
- Enemy or tension: [Open Question — will refine through angle extraction]
- Promise: Actionable advice that makes you better — not inspiration that makes you feel better.
- Emotional tone: Blunt and experienced as the default. Aggressive/confrontational tones used aspirationally ("this is what it takes"), not just to tear down. Never cruel, never preachy.
- What the account should never say: Generic motivational fluff, empty platitudes, AI-sounding filler, anything that sounds like a poster in a middle school gym
