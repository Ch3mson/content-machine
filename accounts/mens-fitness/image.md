Product context: w(inner) should look like a premium athlete mental performance tool. Product visuals should feel native to sport routines, not like app-store advertising.

## Product Visual Direction

Use this as default guidance until design references override it.

- Product feel: premium, restrained, dark, high-end, calm.
- Product context: locker room, team bus, hotel room, sideline, training facility, tunnel, bedroom night-before, recovery day.
- Product role: part of a mental routine, not the hero unless the competitor flow is product-led.
- Product visuals: app-in-hand images are preferred over floating screenshots when the goal is native-feeling content.
- Avoid wellness cliches: candles, lotus poses, pastel meditation visuals, generic calm gradients.
- Avoid ad cliches: phone mockup floating over a background, download badges, hard CTA buttons, exaggerated glow around the app.

## Design Reference Boundary

Borrow:

- Typography hierarchy.
- Layout logic.
- Spacing behavior.
- Overlay mechanics.
- Crop strategy.
- Texture and contrast principles.
- Slide-to-slide consistency patterns.

Do not borrow:

- Exact composition.
- Exact brand identity.
- Exact images.
- Watermarks.
- Proprietary templates.

## Slide Role Visual Rules

These rules must stay aligned with `flow.md`.

| Slide role | Visual job | Typography rule | Image rule | Overlay rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hero | Stop the scroll. | Largest type and strongest hierarchy. | Most dramatic crop or subject. | Use sparingly. |
| List item | Deliver one point. | Clear and repeatable. | Support the point without competing. | Repeat consistent labels or marks. |
| Proof | Make a claim feel earned. | Smaller, precise, less emotional. | Use evidence-like or behavior-specific visuals. | Use annotations only when they clarify. |
| Contrast | Show before, after, wrong, or right. | Use hierarchy instead of cliche copy formulas. | Use visual contrast if the flow supports it. | Avoid clutter. |
| Closing | Leave a useful takeaway. | Calm, legible, final. | Can be simpler than the hero. | Soft app context only if native. |

## Product Asset Rules

- Product imagery is optional.
- App-in-hand images can appear only when they help the slide feel more concrete.
- Do not make the slideshow look like an ad.
- Do not use product screenshots as decoration.
- If a product image appears, the reason must be documented in `flow.md` or `extractions/flow-analysis.md`.
- Product assets should show w(inner) as part of preparation: before sleep, before training, before competition, or after a mistake.
- Product screenshots may be useful for radar chart, track summary, or audio generation concepts, but only when the slide role asks for product explanation.
- App-in-hand imagery can work as proof of routine, especially when the reference design already uses real-life objects or documentary-style crops.

## Visual QA Checklist

- Does the visual rule respect the slide role from `flow.md`?
- Does the text have enough space to breathe?
- Does the image support the copy instead of repeating it?
- Does any app asset feel native?
- Would the reference owner recognize this as copied? If yes, revise.
- Does the visual avoid meditation, therapy, and wellness-app signals?
- Does product imagery feel like part of athlete preparation rather than an ad?
