# Writing Source Of Truth

This file defines the men's fitness account voice and copy rules. It uses `../../references/skills/stop-slop/` as the base anti-AI-writing standard.

Product context: w(inner) is a mental performance app for athletes. Copy should speak to athletes who have trained physically but still hesitate, spiral, overthink, or underperform when the moment matters.

## Base Skill

Use Stop Slop before delivering copy:

- `../../references/skills/stop-slop/SKILL.md`
- `../../references/skills/stop-slop/references/phrases.md`
- `../../references/skills/stop-slop/references/structures.md`
- `../../references/skills/stop-slop/references/examples.md`

Core inherited rules:

- Cut throat-clearing.
- Remove filler and adverbs.
- Avoid binary contrast formulas.
- Avoid dramatic fragments.
- Use active voice.
- Name the specific thing.
- Put the reader in the room.
- Vary rhythm.
- Trust readers.

## Account Voice

The voice should feel:

- Direct.
- Practical.
- Specific.
- Calm.
- Unimpressed by generic motivation.
- Premium and restrained.
- Like a private performance coach, not a wellness influencer.

The voice should not feel:

- Motivational-poster.
- Fitness guru.
- Bro-science.
- Overexplained.
- Sponsored.
- AI-polished.
- Therapeutic.
- Clinical.
- Meditation-app generic.
- Hustle-culture loud.

## Slideshow Copy Rules

- One idea per slide.
- Use short lines, but avoid fake dramatic fragmentation.
- Lead with concrete claims, mistakes, cues, or mechanisms.
- Use second person when it puts the viewer in the scene.
- Use first person only when the account has a real point of view or lived observation.
- Prefer plain verbs over abstract nouns.
- Avoid sweeping claims unless a source or product proof supports them.
- Treat each slide as a visual caption, not an essay paragraph.
- Make the athlete feel the exact moment: tunnel, free throw line, first tee, walkout, trial, penalty, last rep, final minute.
- Prefer sport-specific pressure over broad "mindset" language.
- If the copy mentions w(inner), keep it soft and routine-based.

## Hook Rules

Good hooks:

- Name a specific fitness mistake.
- Promise a practical correction.
- Create useful tension.
- Make the viewer feel seen without flattering them.
- Name the exact mental performance gap: hesitation, overthinking, pre-game dread, slump loops, clutch freezing, or confidence dips.
- Tie the hook to a moment athletes recognize.

Avoid hooks that:

- Start with "Here is why" or "The truth is".
- Use vague stakes.
- Depend on shock without utility.
- Sound like a quote graphic.
- Sound like therapy marketing.
- Promise guaranteed confidence or performance.

## Body Slide Rules

- Each body slide should explain one reason, cue, mistake, or mechanism.
- Use examples from training behavior, daily habits, decision points, or body feedback.
- Avoid generic "discipline" language unless the slide names a specific behavior.
- If a claim sounds scientific, check `sources.md` or flag it.
- Use sport, position, opponent, venue, trigger, or scenario details when available.
- Explain mental reps through specific moments, not abstract psychology.

## Closing Slide Rules

- End with a useful takeaway.
- Do not force a hard CTA.
- A soft app mention is allowed only if `flow.md` supports it.
- If the app mention feels sponsored, move the app angle to comments or bio.
- Good endings leave the athlete with a routine, question, cue, or rehearsal target.

## Banned Patterns For This Account

- "Here is the thing"
- "Let that sink in"
- "The uncomfortable truth"
- "This matters because"
- "In today's fitness landscape"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Crush your goals"
- "Transform your body"
- "Discipline beats motivation"
- "No excuses"
- "Not X, but Y" as a default structure
- "Cure anxiety"
- "Fix your confidence"
- "Never choke again"
- "Manifest the win"
- "Meditate your way to success"
- "Clinically proven" unless proof is added

## Product Language Rules

Use:

- mental performance
- visualization audio
- mental reps
- match-day prep
- athlete identity
- sport, position, and specific moment
- Confidence, Focus, Vision, Resilience, Composure, Drive

Avoid:

- therapy
- treatment
- cure
- diagnosis
- meditation app
- wellness app
- guaranteed win
- scholarship guarantee

## Product Mention Rules

- Product copy should sound like part of an athlete's routine.
- Mention w(inner) only when the flow supports a native slot.
- If a claim mentions confidence, anxiety, sleep, injury, or performance improvement, check `../../product/claim-bank.md`.
- Strong claims should be marked "flagged for approval" unless already approved.
- Do not write hard CTAs in the slideshow.
