# Men's Fitness Account Brief

Use this file to capture the conversational onboarding for the men's fitness pilot account.

## Account Basics

- Account name: Men's fitness pilot
- Niche: athletic men's fitness and mental performance
- Target audience: athletic men and athletes who care about performance under pressure
- Platform priority: TikTok and Instagram share the same rules in v1
- Primary performance goal: saves and shares
- Product relationship to w(inner): soft product context only when competitor flow supports a native routine, preparation, or mental performance slot

## Audience Understanding

- What the audience wants: perform closer to their ability when it matters
- What the audience struggles with: hesitation, overthinking, self-doubt, pressure, slumps, and confidence dips
- What they already believe: physical training matters, but their mind can still get in the way
- What they are tired of hearing: generic motivation, discipline slogans, hustle language, therapy-flavored wellness advice
- What would make them save/share: specific performance moments, practical routines, recognizable pressure scenarios, identity-level reframes
- What would make the account feel fake: hard app CTAs, guaranteed outcomes, clinical claims, generic gym transformation copy

## Account Point Of View

- Core belief: many athletes have enough physical ability, but they need a better way to rehearse the mental moments that decide performance
- Enemy or tension: talent that disappears under pressure
- Promise: show athletes how to prepare for the moments where they usually hesitate
- Emotional tone: calm, direct, premium, performance-coach-like
- What the account should never say: "click link in bio," "this fixes anxiety," "never choke again," "transform your body," or "no excuses"

## Product Mention Decision

- Default product visibility: invisible or implied unless the competitor flow creates a native slot
- Best native placement: preparation routine, mental reps, match-day ritual, reflection, or app-in-hand context
- Placements to avoid: hard slideshow CTA, floating product ad, unsupported performance guarantee
- Claims approved: see `../../product/claim-bank.md`
- Claims flagged: confidence, anxiety, sleep, injury-return, and performance-improvement claims
