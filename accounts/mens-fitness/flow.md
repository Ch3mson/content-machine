# Flow Source Of Truth

This file defines the reusable slideshow structure for the men's fitness account. It should be updated only after reviewing `extractions/flow-analysis.md`.

Product context: this account promotes w(inner), a mobile mental performance app for athletes. The account can use men's fitness aesthetics, but content should lean toward athletic performance, pressure, confidence, identity, and competition preparation rather than generic gym transformation.

- Primary goal: saves and shares.
- Default platforms: TikTok and Instagram use the same structure in v1.
- Product stance: soft mention only when native to the competitor-derived format.
- Product fit: strongest when the slideshow topic involves confidence, pressure, visualization, self-doubt, mental reps, slumps, match-day routines, or athlete identity.

## Product-Relevant Flow Angles

Use these only when the competitor-derived format supports them.

- Talent-performance gap: the athlete has skill but cannot access it when pressure hits.
- Game-day confidence: the athlete needs a repeatable pre-competition routine.
- Slump breaker: one mistake has started becoming part of the athlete's identity.
- Mental reps: the athlete can rehearse decisions, technique, or presence while resting, injured, traveling, or preparing.
- Match-day prep: the athlete needs a night-before anchor or pre-game activation.
- Athlete identity: the athlete wants to become the version of themselves who asks for the moment.

Avoid turning every slideshow into product education. The viewer should get value even if w(inner) is never mentioned.

## Pacing Rules

- Slide 1 must make a concrete promise.
- Each middle slide should add one distinct reason, mistake, cue, or mechanism.
- Do not use filler transition slides.
- Keep the final slide useful even if the viewer never reads the caption.
- Favor save-worthy utility over motivational finishers.

## Soft App Mention Rules

- No hard in-slideshow CTA by default.
- Do not write "click link in bio" inside the slideshow.
- The app can appear only when it fits the extracted competitor flow.
- If the competitor flow has no native CTA slot, generate three hypotheses before using the app.
- The soft mention can be moved to comments, caption, or bio when it would make the slideshow feel sponsored.
- Use product language from `../../product/app-brief.md`.
- Check `../../product/claim-bank.md` before using performance, confidence, sleep, anxiety, or outcome claims.
- Never imply w(inner) is therapy, a meditation app, a clinical tool, or a guaranteed performance fix.

## Native Soft Mention Examples

These are examples of placement types, not final copy.

- Final slide routine note: "Before the game, rehearse the exact moment you usually rush."
- Caption or comment context: "I am testing this idea inside w(inner) as a match-day visualization track."
- Visual prop only: an app-in-hand image appears as part of a locker-room or travel routine when the design reference supports product-in-context imagery.

Avoid:

- "Download w(inner)."
- "Click the link in bio."
- "This app will fix your confidence."
- Product screenshots dropped into the slideshow without structural reason.
