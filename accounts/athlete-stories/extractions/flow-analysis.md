## Extracted Mechanics

- **Hook type**: No traditional hook — jumps straight into a strong fact
- **Slide count**: 6-7 slides
- **Slide roles**: Each slide = one sentence, one fact. No multi-point slides
- **Pacing**: Fast. Each slide is a standalone punch
- **Tension curve**: Builds through escalating facts about dedication, peaks at visualization slide, closes with underdog origin
- **Visualization placement**: Slide 4 of 6 — buried in the middle as just another fact, not highlighted as THE thing
- **CTA**: None visible in captured slides
- **Design**: Full-bleed photo + centered white text with black outline. Alternates B&W and color. No branding, no logos
- **What changes**: Photo mood shifts between intense/training and triumphant moments

## Proposed Flow Structure (7 slides)

1. **The Low Point** — Where the athlete was before the transformation (struggle, doubt, near-quitting)
2. **The Grind** — The physical work that wasn't enough on its own
3. **The Breaking Point** — A moment of failure or crisis that forced change
4. **The Discovery** — Visualization/mental training as the turning point (PEAK)
5. **The Transformation** — How mental training changed their performance
6. **The Product Interjection** — Soft w(inner) mention woven into the narrative
7. **The Quote** — Athlete's own words that wrap the story
