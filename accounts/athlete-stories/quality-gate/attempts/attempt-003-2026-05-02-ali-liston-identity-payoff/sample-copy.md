# Sample Copy: Ali vs. Liston — The Round He Called

## Slide 1 — The Monster

They called Liston a monster.
Mob ties. Prison record.
He had flattened Floyd Patterson —
a former heavyweight champion —
in 124 seconds.

## Slide 2 — The Boy

Clay was 22.
His hands were small.
The odds said he would not survive three rounds.

## Slide 3 — The Antics

At the weigh-in, Clay went wild.
"I'm gonna get you, sucker!"
Reporters laughed. The commission threatened a fine.
Liston never blinked.

## Slide 4 — The Game

But that was the game.
Clay needed Liston angry.
An angry Liston comes forward.
A forward Liston is there
when the round comes.

## Slide 5 — The Night

Three nights before the fight.
2 AM. Liston's motel.
Clay walks onto the lawn
in a field jacket and screams.

## Slide 6 — The Chant

"Round seven. Round seven. Round seven."
Over and over until the floodlights came on.
Guests watching from windows.
A 22-year-old screaming
like he was predicting the future
at a man who had never lost.

## Slide 7 — What They Called It

Reporters called it a breakdown.
Fear, they said. The moment caught up to him.

## Slide 8 — What It Was

He was planting the future.
Until his mind and body believed
the words he shouted.

## Slide 9 — The Payoff

Round seven. Liston does not answer the bell.
The exact round Clay had been screaming
into the Chicago night.

## Slide 10 — The Identity

Before Clay was Muhammad Ali,
he kept rehearsing these moments.
Shouted words into existence
until he shaped his identity
into something unstoppable.
The mind forces the body to change.

## Slide 11 — The Bridge

I use w(inner) the same way.
It builds personalized visualization tracks
so I can imagine myself winning,
performing at my potential.
I rewired my brain so my body followed.

## Slide 12 — The Quote

"I figured I whooped him
in my mind
before the fight ever started."
— Muhammad Ali

## Copy Notes

- Fact-led opening (Slide 1): no throat-clearing, no "before the fame," no "we all know Liston."
- Slippery slope: each slide answers the question created by the previous one.
- Slide 4 is the strategic reveal — the weigh-in antics were calculated, not random.
- Slides 5-6 build the suspense of the hotel incident without explaining it yet.
- Slides 7-8 are the misinterpretation → correction two-step: one slide for what they said, one for what was true.
- Slide 9 is the concrete payoff: the exact round happened.
- Slide 10 is the identity reveal (the "mental turn"): he shaped his identity through rehearsal.
- Slide 11 is the product bridge, first-person, earned by 10 slides of proof.
- Slide 12 is the quote closer — no commentary after it.
- No generic motivation, no banned phrases, no flat fact lists.
- Every slide is 2-5 short lines — readable at 36pt centered on 1080x1920.
