# Account Brief: Athlete Stories

## Account Basics

- Account name: athlete-stories
- Niche: Athlete origin stories and mental visualization
- Target audience: Aspiring athletes, sports fans, young competitors
- Platform priority: TikTok / Instagram Reels (slideshow format)
- Primary performance goal: Saves/shares, product awareness for w(inner)
- Product relationship to w(inner): Soft native mention on Slide 6 (The Product). Must feel organic to the athlete's visualization habit. Never forced.

## Audience Understanding

- What the audience wants: Inspiration rooted in real struggle, not hype
- What the audience struggles with: Self-doubt, pressure, access to mental training
- What they already believe: Great athletes visualize and prepare mentally
- What they are tired of hearing: Generic motivation, "grind" culture, empty hustle
- What would make them save/share: A specific fact or quote that reframes a known athlete
- What would make the account feel fake: Over-polished design, salesy product mentions, generic inspiration language

## Account Point Of View

- Core belief: The gap between good and great is mental, and it starts before the arena.
- Enemy or tension: Untapped potential, physical-first training culture, pressure without preparation
- Promise: Every post shows how a legendary athlete built the mental habit that made them unstoppable.
- Emotional tone: Blunt, grounded, aspirational. No hype.
- What the account should never say: Generic wellness language, clinical claims, guaranteed outcomes, "before the fame" throat-clearing.

## Product Mention Decision

- Default product visibility: Soft mention on Slide 6 only
- Best native placement: First-person statement connecting athlete's visualization habit to w(inner)
- Placements to avoid: Slide 1 (hook), Slide 7 (quote), forced insertions
- Claims approved: "builds a visualization track," "personalized for my sport and matchup," "mental reps"
- Claims flagged: Any performance guarantee, medical claim, replacement for coaching

## Research Decisions

| Content pillar | Claim risk | Source type needed | User notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Athlete origin stories | Low | Biography/autobiography | Factual claims about childhood/struggle |
| Visualization habits | Medium | Interview/primary source | Claims about mental rehearsal routines |
| Product claims | High | Product brief/claim bank | Must map to approved w(inner) claims only |
