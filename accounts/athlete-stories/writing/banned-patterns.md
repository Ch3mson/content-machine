# Banned Patterns: Athlete Stories

Use this file for account-specific weak moves, banned phrasing, and replacements.

## Banned Moves

| Pattern | Why It Fails | Replacement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening with "Before the fame" | Throat-clearing delays the proof. | Start with the origin fact or pressure moment. |
| Generic "no excuses" pressure | Sounds copied from discipline pages and can feel cheap. | Name the actual condition the athlete endured. |
| Slide-as-lesson | Turns the carousel into a lecture. | Make the athlete behavior carry the lesson. |
| Unsourced extreme routine claim | Breaks trust and creates fact risk. | Source it, soften it, or use public record instead. |
| Product feature list | Makes the post feel sponsored. | One first-person line that mirrors the athlete's mental method. |
| Overexplained mental state | Invents what the athlete felt. | Use observable behavior or sourced quote. |
| Flat fact list | Facts may be true but the reader feels no suspense or payoff. | Arrange facts as pressure, identity shift, impact, then bridge. |
| Product before payoff | w(inner) appears before the reader sees why mental rehearsal matters. | Show the impact of the athlete's imagination or mentality first. |
| Quote plus narrator commentary | Weakens the athlete's own words. | End on the quote. |
| Competitor slide-for-slide copy | Produces imitation instead of account voice. | Extract the mechanism, then rebuild around athlete-stories structure. |

## Banned Phrases

- Before the fame
- Let that sink in
- No excuses
- What's your excuse today?
- That's when everything changed
- The real secret was
- He did not just train
- Built different
- Beast mode
- Killer mentality
- Unlock your potential
- Guaranteed confidence
- Cures anxiety
- Meditation app

## Weak Structures

- "Not X, but Y" contrast lines.
- Three-slide lists that repeat the same sentence shape.
- Long educational paragraphs with no new visual proof.
- Hook questions that do not name an athlete-specific pressure.
- Any product slide that says or implies w(inner) made the athlete great.

## Cleanup Checklist

- [ ] Remove generic motivation.
- [ ] Remove empty throat-clearing.
- [ ] Remove unsupported claims.
- [ ] Remove copy that sounds like a template instead of the account.
- [ ] Remove em dashes from final slide copy.
- [ ] Remove adverbs unless they are inside a sourced quote.
- [ ] Verify product claims against `product/claim-bank.md`.
