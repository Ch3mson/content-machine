# Writing QA: Athlete Stories

Use this file before approving copy samples, `flow.md`, or final post copy.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The first slide starts with a specific athlete fact, pressure, or direct challenge.
- [ ] The copy follows `writing.md` current rules.
- [ ] The copy reflects `writing/principles.md`.
- [ ] The copy passes the three-persona check in `writing/refinement-personas.md`.
- [ ] No banned pattern from `writing/banned-patterns.md` appears.
- [ ] Each slide has one clear job.
- [ ] Each slide creates momentum into the next slide instead of sitting as an isolated fact.
- [ ] The mental turn shows identity, imagination, visualization, or self-belief before public outcome.
- [ ] The product bridge comes after the impact of the mental mechanism is clear.
- [ ] The copy fits the current account `design.md` layout and visual-density constraints.
- [ ] Athlete routine, visualization, and claim-heavy facts are sourced, marked for verification, or removed.
- [ ] Product mentions use only approved claims from `product/claim-bank.md` and `product/app-brief.md`.
- [ ] The product line feels like a native training behavior, not an ad.
- [ ] The quote closer has no narrator commentary after it.

## Scoring

Rate each 1-10. Below 50/70: revise before approval. Any claim-safety failure blocks approval even if the score is high.

## Copy Fit Test

Before rendering, check every slide:

1. Can the slide be understood in under three seconds?
2. Does it reveal one new proof point?
3. Does it make the next slide feel necessary?
4. Would the line still work without the photo?
5. Does the photo make the line feel more believable?
6. Is the claim safe enough for final use?
