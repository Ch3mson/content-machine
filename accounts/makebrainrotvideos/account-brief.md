# Account Brief

## Account Basics

- Account name: makebrainrotvideos
- Niche: AI video generation / make money online
- Target audience: People who want to mass-generate viral AI videos to make
  internet money — selling products, promoting apps, making avatars, building
  faceless brands.
- Platform priority: TikTok + Instagram
- Primary performance goal: Brand awareness (get MakeBrainrotVideos.com
  recognized as the go-to AI video generator)
- Product relationship: Direct promotion of MakeBrainrotVideos.com. See
  `../../product/makebrainrotvideos-brief.md`.

## Audience Understanding

- What the audience wants: Fast, scalable ways to make money online using AI.
  They want volume, speed, and proof that it works.
- What the audience struggles with: Finding tools that actually scale. Most AI
  tools make one video at a time. They want to mass-produce, not hand-craft.
- What they already believe: AI content is the future. Money is there to be made.
  They just need the right tool and strategy.
- What they are tired of hearing: Generic "start a side hustle" advice. Fake guru
  promises. Tools that oversell and underdeliver.
- What would make them save/share: Specific earnings proof, step-by-step
  breakdowns, "I made X in Y days" reveals, before/after screenshots.
- What would make the account feel fake: Polished corporate tone, vague claims
  without numbers, motivational fluff without actionable steps.

## Account Point Of View

- Core belief: Volume wins. The creators who test the most concepts the fastest
  are the ones who go viral and make real money.
- Enemy or tension: Slow, manual content creation. One-video-at-a-time tools.
  The old way of editing videos by hand.
- Promise: You can mass-generate viral AI content and actually make money from it.
- Emotional tone: Mix of first-person flex ("I made $15k/month") and
  educational how-to ("How people are making $15k/month with AI"). Bold,
  direct, money-motivated, slightly provocative.
- What the account should never say: Generic motivational quotes. Corporate
  marketing language. "Just believe in yourself." Anything that sounds like a
  wellness brand.

## Angle Variant Preferences

- Strongest desire levers: Making money fast, scaling without effort, proof of
  results, "secret tool" framing.
- Weak angle patterns to avoid: Generic motivation, vague "change your life"
  claims, long explanations without payoff, and purely educational "idea generation".
- Identity gaps that resonate: "I was broke / now I make $X" transformation.
  Age-flex ("at 14", "at 19"). Speed-flex ("in 30 days").
- Viral curiosity patterns that fit the account: Income reveals, tool reveals,
  "what nobody tells you" framings, step-by-step breakdowns.
- Example winning variant: "how I made $15k/month at 14" with cash-on-table
  imagery.
- **Performance Note (2026-05-09):** "Pain Point / Harsh Truth" angles (e.g., "stuck at 200 views") significantly outperform purely "Educational" angles (e.g., "faceless content ideas"). The audience responds strongly to algorithm hacks and solutions to their immediate frustrations.

## Product Mention Decision

- Default product visibility: Visible. This account exists to promote the tool.
- Best native placement: Last 1-2 slides as a CTA, or woven into how-to steps
  as the tool being demonstrated.
- Placements to avoid: Opening slide product pitch (kills engagement). Every
  slide mentioning the product (feels like an ad).
- Claims approved: See `../../product/makebrainrotvideos-claim-bank.md`
- Claims flagged: Income guarantees, "no effort" framing.

## Research Decisions

| Content pillar | Claim risk | Source type needed | User notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Income proof | High — FTC scrutiny on earnings claims | Verified creator earnings or qualified framing | Always qualify with "top creators" or "results vary" |
| AI video quality | Medium — quality is subjective | Platform examples, view counts | Use real view counts from the platform |
| Tool comparison | Medium — competitor mention risk | Feature-based, not name-based | Compare categories, not specific competitors |
