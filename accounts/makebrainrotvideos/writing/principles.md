# Writing Principles

Stable account-specific principles extracted from references. These override
`pattern-extractions.md` and `copywriting-principles.md` when they conflict.

## Source Summary

Extracted from @wealth_builders_x (30+ posts, 6K followers, 54.9K likes) and
@money.vendor0 (money-flex slideshows). Weighted toward the top 5 slideshow
posts by engagement (15.8K–24.2K likes each, plus 1.5M viral outlier).

## Core Principles

| Principle | Rule | Source |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lead with a number | Every hook needs a specific dollar amount, count, or time frame. "$2,000/month" beats "make money online." "5 Ways" beats "Ways to get rich." | 6+ top posts use number hooks. |
| One idea per slide | Each slide delivers exactly one step, one claim, or one item. If you need a comma, you need a second slide. | All top-performing posts follow this. |
| 5-7 slides per post | Under 5 feels incomplete. Over 8 loses swipe momentum. The sweet spot is 5-7. | Consistent across top posts. |
| Fragments over sentences | Slides use phrases, not sentences. "Step 1: Find a niche" not "The first step is to find a niche that works for you." | All reference posts. |
| Caption is the CTA | "Comment for more," "Save for later," "Swipe to learn." The caption drives engagement. The slides deliver value. | Top 3 posts by engagement all use caption CTAs. |
| Name real tools | Say "ElevenLabs" not "an AI voiceover tool." Say "CapCut" not "a video editor." Specificity signals insider knowledge. | Tool-mention posts average higher saves. |
| Show proof, not promises | Screenshots of earnings, platform dashboards, real tool UIs. Never "imagine making $10K." Show the $10K. | @money.vendor0 reseller proof, @wealth_builders_x tutorial screenshots. |
| Progressive disclosure | Each slide must create a reason to swipe to the next. Never frontload the full answer on slide 1. | All top slideshow posts. |
| Product mention goes last | MakeBrainrotVideos.com appears in the last 1-2 slides or not at all. Never open with the product. | Derived from account brief product placement rules. |
| Ride trending formats | "I leaked," "Mission: Get Rich" (GTA), difficulty ratings. Borrow attention from formats people already engage with. | Trend-jacked posts perform 2-5x above baseline. |

## Voice and Rhythm

- **Tone**: Direct, confident, slightly cocky. Talk like someone who already made
  the money and is casually telling you how. Not preachy. Not salesy.
- **Sentence length**: 3-8 words per slide line. Kill anything longer.
- **Rhythm**: Short, punchy, fast. Every slide should feel like a swipe reward.
- **Slang**: Light internet/hustle slang is fine ("bread," "go crazy," "no cap").
  Do not force it. Do not use cringe slang.
- **Person**: Mix of first-person flex ("I made $15K/month") and second-person
  tutorial ("Step 1: Find a niche"). Never third-person formal.
- **Authority**: "I did this" or "here's how." Never "studies show" or "experts
  say." The account IS the expert.

## Open Questions

- How much slang is too much? Calibrate after copy samples.
- Should tutorial posts always use numbered steps, or can some use other
  structures (before/after, tool comparison)?
- What is the right ratio of flex posts to tutorial posts?
