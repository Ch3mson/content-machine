# Banned Patterns

Account-specific weak moves and phrases. If any of these appear in a draft,
rewrite before approval.

## Banned Moves

| Move | Why it fails | Replace with |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening with a definition | "Affiliate marketing is..." kills the hook. The reader does not want a lesson, they want a result. | Open with a result or proof: "$2,000/month from affiliate links." |
| Multi-sentence slides | Paragraphs on slides signal blog content, not native social. Reader swipes past. | One fragment per slide. One idea. One line. |
| Rhetorical questions as hooks | "Want to make money online?" is the weakest possible hook. Everyone wants money. It says nothing. | Replace with a specific claim or command: "$2,000/month with AI videos" or "Stop editing videos manually." |
| Motivational filler | "Believe in yourself," "Your time is now," "Chase your dreams." Zero engagement on reference accounts. | Cut entirely or replace with a concrete step. |
| Explaining before revealing | "In this post I'll show you..." wastes slide 1. The reader already swiped; just show them. | Jump straight to Step 1 or the first reveal. |
| Product pitch on slide 1 | Opening with "Use MakeBrainrotVideos.com" makes it an ad. Engagement drops. | Product appears on last 1-2 slides only, after value is delivered. |
| Vague income claims | "Make money," "earn income," "build wealth." Too generic to stop a scroll. | Use specific amounts: "$2,000/month," "$940 sale," "$15K." |
| Formal/corporate tone | "We are pleased to announce," "In this comprehensive guide." Sounds like a press release. | Write like a text to a friend who asked how you made money. |

## Banned Phrases

| Phrase | Why | Alternative |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "In this post/video" | Meta-commentary. Reader does not care about the medium. | Cut. Start with the content. |
| "Let me explain" | Sounds like a lecture. | Cut. Just explain. |
| "Studies show" / "Research indicates" | Wrong authority style. This account IS the proof. | "I tested this" or "Here's what works." |
| "Financial freedom" | Overused guru phrase. Triggers scam radar. | "Make $X/month" or "Quit your 9-5." |
| "Passive income guaranteed" | FTC risk + scam signal. | "Potential to earn $X with [method]." |
| "No experience needed" | Sounds like an infomercial. | "I started with zero followers" (proof-based). |
| "Click the link" | Platform-penalized CTA on TikTok. | "Link in bio" or "Check the bio." |
| "Subscribe for more" | YouTube CTA on TikTok. Wrong platform. | "Follow for more" or "Comment for Part 2." |
| "Game-changer" | Overused. Means nothing. | Name what specifically changed. |
| "Unlock your potential" | Wellness/coaching language. Wrong audience. | "Start making money with [tool]." |

## Weak Structures

| Structure | Problem | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hook → 5 slides of explanation → CTA | Middle slides are too dense. Loses swipe momentum. | Hook → Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Proof → CTA. Each slide is one beat. |
| Every slide starts with "Step X" | Monotonous. Reader zones out. | Vary: "Step 1" / "Then" / "The key part" / "Finally." |
| Ending with "Follow for more" | Weak close. No payoff. | End with the biggest reveal, then product or method CTA. |
| All text, no visual cues | Wall of text on slides gets skipped. | Pair text with relevant imagery, screenshots, or tool UIs. |

## Cleanup Checklist

Before approving any draft:

- [ ] No slide has more than one sentence.
- [ ] No banned phrase appears anywhere.
- [ ] Hook has a specific number or command.
- [ ] Product mention is on last 1-2 slides only (if present).
- [ ] No slide sounds like a LinkedIn post.
- [ ] No unqualified income guarantees.
- [ ] Caption contains the engagement CTA, not the slides.
