# Refinement Personas

Three-persona refinement protocol. Every copy draft must pass all three before
approval. If any persona blocks, rewrite before proceeding.

## Persona 1: The Broke Scroller

### Invariant Job

Catches copy that fails to stop the scroll. If this persona keeps scrolling,
the hook is dead.

### Account-Specific Calibration

- Age: 16-24.
- Context: Scrolling TikTok at 11pm, half-watching, thumb hovering over swipe.
- Mindset: "I want to make money but everything sounds like a scam."
- Sees 50+ "make money online" posts per day. Immune to generic promises.
- Will stop for: specific numbers, proof screenshots, something they have not
  seen before, a bold claim that feels real.
- Will skip: motivational quotes, vague "change your life" hooks, anything that
  looks like an ad, anything longer than 6 words in the hook.

### Pass Criteria

- Hook creates a gut reaction in under 2 seconds: curiosity, disbelief, or greed.
- First slide makes the viewer think "wait, how?"
- No slide makes the viewer think "I've seen this before."

### Blocking Failures

- Hook is generic ("Make money online in 2024").
- First slide is a definition or explanation instead of a hook.
- Any slide feels like a LinkedIn post or corporate content.
- Copy sounds like a teacher, not a peer.

### Non-Blocking Improvements

- Hook could be more specific (add a number or tool name).
- Slang could feel more natural.
- CTA could be more engaging.

### Rewrite Actions

- Replace vague hooks with specific numbers or tool names.
- Cut any slide that explains instead of reveals.
- Make slide 1 impossible to skip.

## Persona 2: The Account-Native Scroller

### Invariant Job

Catches copy that does not match the account's voice. If this persona thinks
"this doesn't feel like @wealth_builders_x," the copy is off-brand.

### Account-Specific Calibration

- Follows @wealth_builders_x, @money.vendor0, and similar hustle accounts.
- Expects: short punchy slides, luxury/money imagery context, numbered steps,
  fragment-style copy, imperative CTAs.
- Compares against these sentence-flow traits:
  - **Hook shape**: Number or command in first 5 words.
  - **Sentence rhythm**: 3-8 words per line. No compound sentences.
  - **Line length**: Never wraps to a third line on mobile.
  - **Pacing**: Each slide faster than the last. No slow explanatory slides.
  - **Problem-to-method-to-tool movement**: Slide 1 = hook/problem. Slides 2-5 =
    method/steps. Slide 6-7 = tool or CTA.
  - **Density per slide**: One idea. One visual. One line of text max.
  - **Smoothness**: Each slide logically follows the previous. No jumps.
  - **Swipe momentum**: Every slide ends with an implicit "and then..."

### Pass Criteria

- Copy reads like it belongs on the same feed as @wealth_builders_x's top posts.
- Slide rhythm matches: short, punchy, one-idea-per-slide.
- CTA feels native to the format (caption CTA, not slide CTA).

### Blocking Failures

- Any slide has more than one sentence.
- Copy uses formal language ("In this tutorial, we will explore...").
- Slide structure does not follow progressive disclosure.
- Post exceeds 8 slides or falls below 5.
- Copy reads like a blog post compressed into slides.

### Non-Blocking Improvements

- Slides could have more swipe momentum.
- Could use a trending format or trend-jack.
- Tool names could be more specific.

### Rewrite Actions

- Split any multi-idea slide into separate slides.
- Replace formal language with fragment-style copy.
- Add swipe momentum cues between slides.

## Persona 3: The Bullshit Detector

### Invariant Job

Catches copy that makes the account look like a scam. If this persona thinks
"this is fake," the account loses credibility.

### Account-Specific Calibration

- Has been burned by fake gurus before. Tried a "make money online" method that
  did not work.
- Red flags: unverified income claims, "no effort required," guaranteed outcomes,
  screenshots that look fabricated, advice from someone who clearly has not done
  it.
- Will trust: specific tool names they can Google, step-by-step methods they can
  verify, realistic income ranges, "results vary" qualifiers, proof from real
  platforms.
- Claim-risk areas:
  - Income claims: Must be qualified ("top creators," "results vary") or backed
    by real screenshots.
  - Tool claims: Must be factually accurate. Do not claim features that do not
    exist.
  - Speed claims: "In seconds" is fine for MakeBrainrotVideos.com (it is true).
    "Overnight success" is not.
- Banned jargon: "passive income guaranteed," "get rich quick," "no work
  required," "financial freedom in 30 days."
- Verification ladder:
  1. Is this claim factually true?
  2. Can the viewer verify it themselves?
  3. Would this claim survive a comment section calling it out?
  4. Does this match the product's actual capabilities?

### Pass Criteria

- Every claim is either factual, qualified, or backed by proof.
- Copy does not trigger "scam" pattern recognition.
- The viewer could try the method and get a realistic result.

### Blocking Failures

- Unqualified income guarantee ("You WILL make $10K").
- Fabricated proof or fake screenshots.
- Claims that contradict the product's actual features.
- "No effort required" framing.
- Copy that sounds like an infomercial.

### Non-Blocking Improvements

- Income claims could be more precisely qualified.
- Could add "results vary" or "top creators" qualifiers.
- Could cite a specific verifiable example.

### Rewrite Actions

- Add qualifiers to income claims.
- Replace guarantees with proof or realistic framing.
- Verify tool feature claims against actual product.
