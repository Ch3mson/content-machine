# Copywriting Principles

General persuasion logic behind the account style. Explains why the writing
principles work. Lower authority than `principles.md`.

## Reader Psychology

- **The scroll is the enemy.** The reader is not reading. They are swiping.
  You have under 2 seconds to earn a stop. Everything after that is borrowed
  time.
- **Greed beats curiosity beats fear.** This audience responds to "here's how
  to make money" more than "here's what you're missing" more than "here's what
  you'll lose." Lead with opportunity.
- **Specificity is credibility.** "$2,000/month with ElevenLabs voiceovers" is
  believable. "Make money with AI" is not. The more specific the claim, the
  more the reader trusts it.
- **Social proof is the shortcut.** Screenshots, view counts, earnings proof,
  tool dashboards. One real screenshot beats ten slides of copy.
- **Identity aspiration drives saves.** People save posts that represent who
  they want to become. "Save for later" works because saving = "I'm the kind
  of person who acts on this."

## Hook Logic

- **Number hooks stop scrolls.** "$15K," "5 Ways," "at 14." Numbers are
  processed faster than words. They create instant specificity.
- **Command hooks create action.** "Comment your favorite," "Save for later,"
  "Stop using X." Imperatives bypass the reader's passive scrolling mode.
- **Curiosity gaps create obligation.** "I leaked," "what nobody tells you,"
  "the tool that replaced X." The brain needs closure.
- **Trend hooks borrow attention.** Formats the reader already engages with
  (GTA mission screens, difficulty ratings, "I leaked" trends) carry built-in
  engagement patterns.

## Flow Logic

- **Progressive disclosure keeps swipes coming.** Each slide reveals one piece.
  The full picture only exists after all slides. This is why listicles and
  step-by-step tutorials dominate.
- **Slide 1 is the hook. Slide 2 is the commitment.** If they swipe once, they
  will usually finish. Slide 2 must reward the swipe with value, not more
  setup.
- **The last slide is the payoff.** Either the final step, the tool reveal, or
  the product CTA. Never end on a weak slide.
- **Momentum > completeness.** It is better to cut a step than to slow the
  rhythm. If a slide feels like filler, delete it.

## Editing Heuristics

- **If a slide has a comma, split it.** Each comma is a potential second slide.
- **If a word can be cut, cut it.** "The best way to start" becomes "Best way
  to start" becomes "Start here."
- **If the hook does not make you react, rewrite it.** The hook is the only
  slide that matters for reach. Spend 50% of writing time on it.
- **If the copy sounds like a teacher, rewrite it.** This account talks like a
  peer who already made it, not a professor explaining theory.
- **If you would not save it, do not post it.** The save rate is the strongest
  signal. If the post has no reference or tutorial value, it will not save.
