## Viewer-Perspective Notes

Why does a viewer stop scrolling?

- **Specific money number in first 5 words.** "$2,000 a month," "$15k a month,"
  "$940 easy bread." Vague "make money" gets skipped.
- **Age or time flex.** "At 14," "14-25 Years Old," "in 2024." Creates disbelief
  or FOMO.
- **Listicle promise.** "5 Ways," "5 Best Business." Reader knows exactly what
  they are getting.
- **Curiosity gap.** "I leaked," "Stop using X, try Y instead." Reader needs the
  answer.
- **CTA as hook.** "Comment your favorite," "Save for later." Engagement
  instruction doubles as stop-scroll.

Why does a viewer swipe through all slides?

- **Progressive disclosure.** Each slide reveals one step or one item. No slide
  gives the full answer.
- **Numbered steps.** "Step 1," "Step 2" creates obligation to finish.
- **Short text per slide.** One sentence or phrase. Never a paragraph.
- **Visual payoff.** Lifestyle imagery (cars, cash, cities) rewards swiping.

Why does a viewer save or share?

- **Tutorial value.** Step-by-step instructions they want to try later.
- **Social currency.** Sharing "I found a secret method" makes the sharer look
  smart.
- **Aspirational identity.** Saving = "I'm the kind of person who will do this."
- **Reference utility.** Lists of tools, business ideas, or methods they want to
  return to.

## Candidate Patterns

| Pattern | Evidence count | Candidate rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Specific dollar amount in hook | 6+ posts | Always lead with a number, not a vague promise. |
| One idea per slide | All top posts | Never put two concepts on one slide. |
| 5-7 slides per post | All top posts | Stay in 5-7 range. Under 5 feels thin, over 8 loses attention. |
| CTA in caption, not last slide | Most top posts | Caption is the CTA. Last slide is the payoff or product mention. |
| Imperative verb hooks | "Comment," "Save," "Stop using," "Start" | Open with a command, not a question or statement. |
| Tool names as specificity | ElevenLabs, CapCut, Submagic, Opus Pro | Name real tools. "AI tool" is too vague. |
| No paragraphs anywhere | All posts | Slides use fragments, phrases, or single sentences. Never multi-sentence. |
| Trend-jacking hooks | "I leaked," "Mission: Get Rich" (GTA style) | Borrow trending formats. Ride existing attention. |

## Rejected Patterns

| Pattern | Why rejected |
| --- | --- |
| Long motivational quotes | Zero engagement on @wealth_builders_x's motivational-only posts. |
| Asking rhetorical questions as hooks | Performs worse than imperative or number hooks. |
| Multiple hashtag lines in slide text | Hashtags belong in caption only, never on slides. |
