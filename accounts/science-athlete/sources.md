# Source Bank: Science Athlete

## Source Policy

- Use sources for high-risk factual claims, specific stats, neuroscience claims, training-transfer claims, and product claims.
- Draft percentages are allowed only when marked `[VERIFY]` and must be replaced or removed before final.
- Do not turn a source into a stronger claim than it supports.
- Use sport-specific research when possible.
- If only cross-sport research exists, frame the claim as a general motor learning or sport psychology principle.
- Product claims must come from `../../product/app-brief.md` or `../../product/claim-bank.md`.

## Claim Risk Map

| Content pillar | Claim risk | Source type needed | Notes |

| Visual scanning before receiving | Medium | Soccer/field-sport visual exploratory activity studies | Safe when framed as linked/associated, not guaranteed |

## Approved Source Categories

- Peer-reviewed sport science papers
- Sport psychology research
- Motor learning and ecological dynamics research
- Sport-specific visual scanning, quiet eye, and decision-making studies
- Meta-analyses and reviews when available
- Official product brief and claim bank for w(inner)

## Source Categories To Avoid

- Unsourced coaching claims presented as science
- TikTok/Instagram trainer claims without citation
- Blog claims without primary source trail
- Medical claims about the brain without support
- Exact percentages from memory unless verified

## Brain Terms: Safe Framing

| Term | Bracket explanation | Safe use | Risky use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Amygdala | the brain's threat-detection system | "Pressure can make the body tighten." | "Your amygdala makes you choke." |
| Hippocampus | the brain area that helps map space and memory | "Scanning gives the brain more information to map space." | "The hippocampus guarantees better first touch." |
| Basal ganglia | brain circuits that store repeated movement patterns | "Repeated rhythm becomes the body's default." | "The basal ganglia causes bad technique." |
| Prefrontal cortex | the brain area involved in planning and decision-making | "Too much late information can overload decision-making." | "Your prefrontal cortex fails." |
| Motor system | the brain-body system that prepares and executes movement | "The body prepares movement from the picture it has." | "Your motor system gets fixed by this drill." |

## Words Or Claims To Avoid

- Guaranteed performance improvement
- Cure, fix anxiety, treat fear, therapy
- Clinically proven mental performance unless proof exists
- Scholarship, contract, selection, or victory guarantees
- Exact neuroscience claims without source
- Invented stats in final copy
