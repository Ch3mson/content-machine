# Account Brief: Science Athlete

## Account Basics

- Account name: science-athlete
- Niche: Sport science, neuroscience, motor learning, and practical performance training for athletes
- Target audience: Ambitious youth, collegiate, and serious amateur athletes who already train but want a smarter edge
- Platform priority: Instagram carousel first, with TikTok slideshow reuse when suitable
- Primary performance goal: Saves/shares first, product awareness second
- Product relationship to w(inner): Soft, native mention only when visualization, mental reps, pressure rehearsal, or scenario rehearsal belongs in the post

## Audience Understanding

- What the audience wants: To feel more fluid, skilled, composed, and game-ready in their sport
- What the audience struggles with: Looking good in drills but feeling stiff, rushed, late, robotic, or uncreative under pressure
- What they already believe: More reps, more drills, more grind, and cleaner technique should make them better
- What they are tired of hearing: Generic motivation, vague hustle advice, overcomplicated science, and drills that do not transfer
- What would make them save/share: A clear explanation of why a familiar problem happens plus a practical fix they can try that day
- What would make the account feel fake: Forced stats, unclear neuroscience, generic athlete language, salesy product plugs, or diagrams that do not explain the action

## Account Point Of View

- Core belief: Skill transfer comes from training the same information the game gives you: pressure, timing, space, visual cues, rhythm changes, and decisions.
- Enemy or tension: Training that looks clean but removes the exact cue the sport requires.
- Promise: Every post names a felt athletic problem, explains the hidden mechanism, and gives a practical correction.
- Emotional tone: Smart, useful, grounded, slightly confrontational, but never confusing.
- What the account should never say: Empty motivation, guaranteed outcomes, medical claims, therapy language, or science terms with no athlete-level explanation.

## Main Reader Feeling

The reader should feel:

> That is exactly what happens to me. Now I know why. I can try this today.

## Product Mention Decision

- Default product visibility: Invisible unless the topic naturally involves mental imagery, visualization, pressure rehearsal, scenario rehearsal, or mental reps.
- Best native placement: Late body slide or close, after the physical/training fix has been established.
- Placements to avoid: Hero slide, early hook, or any slide where the product becomes the main solution before the mechanism is explained.
- Approved product wording: "w(inner) generates personalized visualization audio for your sport, position, and scenario."
- Approved product angle: Mental reps when the body cannot train, pressure-picture rehearsal, position-specific visualization, match-day preparation.
- Claims flagged: Any guarantee that w(inner) improves performance, fixes anxiety, cures fear, prevents choking, wins games, or replaces coaching.
