# Drill Diagram Prompts

Use these prompts in your image generator (Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.) to create the
training diagrams for Slides 2, 4, and 5.

---

## Slide 2 — Cone Drill with Shoulder Check

Create a clean soccer training diagram that an athlete can understand in 3 seconds. Use a top-down coach's whiteboard style on a plain white background, 16:9 aspect ratio.

Show a vertical line of five cones on the left side. Show a single player icon at the end of the cone line. To the right, place two small cone gates — one slightly higher, one slightly lower.

Use purposeful arrows only: dashed arrows show the ball path through the cones, a curved arrow near the player's head shows the shoulder check toward the gates, a solid arrow shows the player receiving across the body, and a bold arrow shows the exit through one gate. Do not add decorative arrows.

Number the steps: 1 dribble through cones, 2 shoulder check, 3 receive across body, 4 exit through gate. Add short athlete-friendly labels.

The diagram should clearly show where the player starts, what they look at, what cue triggers the movement, and where they move next.

## Slide 4 — Scattered Cone Rhythm Drill

Create a clean soccer training diagram that an athlete can understand in 3 seconds. Use a top-down coach's whiteboard style on a plain white background, 16:9 aspect ratio.

Show five cones scattered in a rough circle or random cluster. Place a player icon near one cone. Show two exit gates placed at different angles outside the cluster.

Use purposeful arrows only: a solid arrow shows the player approaching a cone slowly, a small hold/pause marker at the cone, then a bold arrow showing the explosive exit through a random gate. A curved arrow near the player's head shows a quick scan before the burst. Do not add decorative arrows.

Number the steps: 1 slow approach, 2 scan and hold, 3 open hips, 4 explosive exit. Add short athlete-friendly labels.

The diagram should clearly show where the player starts, what they look at, how the rhythm changes, and where they exit.

---

## Slide 5 — Pressure Passing with Shadow Defender

Create a clean soccer training diagram that an athlete can understand in 3 seconds. Use a top-down coach's whiteboard style on a plain white background, 16:9 aspect ratio.

Show a player icon in the center with a ball. Place a partner icon slightly ahead. Place a shadow defender icon (dashed outline or lighter shade) approaching from behind the player at a slight angle. Add two small cone gates as passing targets.

Use purposeful arrows only: dashed arrows show the pass to the partner and the return pass, a curved arrow near the player's head shows a scan for the shadow defender, a solid arrow shows the player's movement to receive the return, and a bold arrow shows the exit after the return touch. Do not add decorative arrows.

Number the steps: 1 pass to partner, 2 scan for shadow, 3 receive return, 4 exit under pressure. Add short athlete-friendly labels.

The diagram should clearly show where the player starts, what pressure cue to watch for, and where they move after receiving.
