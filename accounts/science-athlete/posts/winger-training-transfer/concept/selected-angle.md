# Selected Angle: The Training Version of You Is a Liar

## Selected Angle Issue
Training often removes the exact information sources the game gives you. The winger learned clean technique in a clean environment. The game is not clean. The gap between training self and game self is not a mentality problem — it is an information problem.

## Target Avatar
Soccer winger (youth, collegiate, serious amateur) who dominates in 1v1s and team training but underperforms in matches.

## Pain Point
Feels like two different players: fluid and confident in practice, tense and conservative in games. Skills do not transfer. The work put in does not show up when it counts.

## Promised Self / Desired Identity
The athlete wants their game self to match their training self. They want to feel dangerous and automatic under pressure, not just useful.

## Private Gap
Deep fear that the game version is the "real" version. Training feels like a lie. The identity gap creates self-doubt and status frustration — being seen as a "training player."

## Emotional Lever
Fear of wasted potential + bad training resentment. "I have the tools, but cannot access them when it matters." And: "My training rewarded a fake version of the skill."

## Mechanism In Play
Representative learning design gap. Training removes game-specific information: defender cues, timing pressure, consequence, space reads. The body learned patterns without the variables that make the game a game. Under pressure, the brain defaults to what's automatic — and if the automatic response was learned without game information, it doesn't transfer.

## Fix Direction
1. Stop rehearsing moves. Start rehearsing reads.
2. Add random defender cues, consequence, and game-information to physical training.
3. Use mental imagery (PETTLEP) to fill the gap when physical practice cannot replicate match pressure.

## Claims To Verify Or Avoid
- Pressure/threat response claims: HIGH risk. Frame as "pressure can shrink perceived options" not clinical anxiety.
- Mental imagery claims: MEDIUM risk. Safe when framed as "can support performance, especially when paired with physical practice."
- Do not claim w(inner) fixes anxiety or guarantees transfer.
- Use "linked with" / "can support" language for research claims.

## Words, Frames, Or Tones To Avoid
- "The real secret"
- "Just be confident"
- "Mentality issue"
- "Not X, but Y" repeated
- Generic motivation
- Product as the whole fix
- Dense brain stacking
- Forced stats every slide
- Guaranteed outcomes
