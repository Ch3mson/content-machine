# Intake: Halftime Restart Flatness

## Confirmed Intake

- Account: `athlete-max`
- Target avatar: competitive athletes across sports with halftime, intermission, or break periods.
- Pain point: starting the second half flat, slow, tight, or mentally checked out after feeling ready before the game.
- Desired transformation: come out of halftime already game-speed ready.
- Likely emotional lever: invisible competition — the opponent is staying ready while you cool down.
- Mechanism direction: halftime can reduce readiness through body temperature drop, nervous-system downshift, and attention drift.
- What the post should avoid: motivational discipline talk, bro-science, generic “lock in” advice, and overclaiming that halftime always causes performance drops.
- Product or claim constraints: no w(inner) mention unless later reframed as a mental reset or visualization routine; factual claims need source support.

## Working Concept Seed

**Why halftime makes good athletes start the second half slow**

The first few minutes after halftime are treated like a restart, but the body may not be in the same state it was during the first half. The post should explain the mechanism and give a practical halftime reset standard.
