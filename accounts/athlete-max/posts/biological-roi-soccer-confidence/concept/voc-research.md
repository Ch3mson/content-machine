## Mechanisms That Explain The Pain
- **Cognitive anxiety vs. somatic anxiety:** Two separate systems. Cognitive = worry thoughts. Somatic = physical tension (tight muscles, shallow breathing). Both degrade performance but through different pathways.
- **Prefrontal cortex override:** Under pressure, the prefrontal cortex (conscious thinking) hijacks the basal ganglia (automatic skill execution). This is why players "overthink" and lose their first touch.
- **HPA axis and cortisol:** Chronic pre-game anxiety keeps cortisol elevated, which impairs working memory and decision-making speed.
- **Fear extinction through exposure:** Repeatedly performing under controlled pressure physically rewires the amygdala-prefrontal cortex connection.
- **Alpha brainwave training:** Neurofeedback studies show elite players can train alpha activity (8-12 Hz) to improve focus, flow state access, and decision-making under pressure.
- **Repeated sprint training reduces anxiety:** A 2018 study found 6 weeks of repeated sprint training significantly reduced both cognitive and somatic anxiety while increasing self-confidence in elite young soccer players.
- **Mindfulness improves attention control:** Frontiers in Psychology (2024) found mindfulness training positively correlates with confidence and negatively with competitive state anxiety during penalty shootouts.

## Claim-Risk Notes
- Avoid claiming any method "guarantees" confidence — frame as "research shows association" or "studies demonstrate"
- Neuroscience claims need source citations (journal name + PMID)
- The repeated sprint training study is real and citable (ScienceDirect, 2018)
- Mindfulness/penalty study is real (Frontiers in Psychology, 2024)
- Neurofeedback study is real (PMC, 2024)
- Avoid overclaiming on "alpha brainwaves" — frame as one tool among several
