The approved quality-gate attempt-007 sample cited "Williams, Hodges & Elliott -
J Sports Sci 1999, PMID: 10408340." PubMed metadata for PMID 10408340 points to
a peptide research article, not a soccer visual-search or sport anticipation
article. This post does not reuse that source line.

## Claim Handling

- The post avoids exact percentage claims.
- The post phrases fatigue effects as "can impair" where source specificity
  matters.
- The scan drill is framed as a practical training cue based on visual scanning
  and peripheral-reaction research, not as a guaranteed outcome.
