## What This Sample Tests

- Whether `writing.md` can make the athlete's payoff instant before teaching the mechanism.
- Whether the copy stays sport-specific to soccer instead of generic athlete language.
- Whether the three-persona protocol in `writing/refinement-personas.md` catches jargon, weak desire translation, and unsupported claim risk before rendering.
- Whether the body-slide system in `design.md` can hold label, title, explanation, fixed `800x450` image frame, lower fix/result section, and source line without moving the frame or shrinking typography below the hierarchy.
- Whether lowercase section labels feel native and readable on a dense non-hero slide.

## Source Handling

`accounts/athlete-max/sources.md` is not present. This sample keeps the science claim marked for verification in the source line and should not be treated as a final sourced post.

## Writing Principles Being Tested

- Problem-first and desire-first copy: the slide names the wanted soccer outcome before the mechanism.
- One reader: a soccer player whose touch and next read fade after repeat sprints.
- Mechanism before protocol: fluid loss → heat/processing cost → hydration fix.
- Performance translation: the mechanism is tied to first touch and next-pass decisions.
- Claim safety: the hydration/cognition claim is softened and marked for verification.

## Design Rules Used

- Canvas: `1080x1350`
- Background: `#FFFFFF`
- Safe margins: at least `72px` left/right and `60px` bottom
- Body image frame: `800x450`, centered at `x=140`, `y=515`
- Typography: Arial regular/bold from Windows fonts
- Body slide alignment: centered label, title, upper explanation, lower fix/result, and source
- Source line: small, centered, visually secondary
