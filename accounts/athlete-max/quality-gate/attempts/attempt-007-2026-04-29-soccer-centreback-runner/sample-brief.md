## What Is Being Tested

- Writing voice and rhythm on a completely new topic (centreback-specific, not first-touch fatigue).
- Information structure: contrarian hook → mechanism → fix → source on a defensive-awareness slide.
- Typography and hierarchy: label + title + explanation + photo + fix + source.

## Topic

Soccer centreback — missing the runner in behind during the final 15 minutes.

## Slide Type

Non-hero body slide (listing format, verified_byhumans Post 1 style).

## Why This Topic

- Centreback-specific (spatial awareness, tracking runners, last-line defending).
- Fresh topic: not a revision of the first-touch fatigue samples from attempts 001–006.
- Tests whether the writing system transfers to a new desire stack (preventing late goals vs. maintaining touch).
- Mechanism is perceptual-cognitive (attention narrowing) rather than purely motor.

## Uncertainty

- Slightly more lines than attempt 006 (6 vs. 5 in upper), requiring recalculated vertical spacing.
- "Behind your shoulder" is soccer-native; broader athlete audience should understand from context.

## Copy Strategy

- Contrarian hook: "your late-game reads are not a reaction problem."
- Mechanism: central fatigue narrows attention around the ball, filtering out the runner behind the shoulder.
- Fix: post-session scanning drill with a verbal constraint.
- Source: Williams, Hodges & Elliott — J Sports Sci 1999.
