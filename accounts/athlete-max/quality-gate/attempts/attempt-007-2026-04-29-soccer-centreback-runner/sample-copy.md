## Top Explanation (center, Arial Regular, 28 px)

```
your late-game reads are not a reaction problem. by minute 70,
central fatigue narrows your attention around the ball so your
brain filters out the runner behind your shoulder. that is why the
runner gets in behind your line in the final 15 minutes even though
you watched the pass and never saw them until the ball was played
and they were already through on goal before you could recover.
```

## Lower Section — Fix + Payoff Embedded (center, Arial Regular, 28 px; labels bold inline)

```
the fix: after your main session, spend 10 minutes scanning from
the ball to the space behind you in a deliberate two-second rhythm.
constraint: you must call out the runner before you turn back to
the ball. this keeps your peripheral vision active, so you still
see the late run in minute 80 when your legs are tired and the
striker expects to slip in behind you without you noticing.
```
