## Top Explanation (center, Arial Regular, 28 px)

```
your late-game touch is not a conditioning problem. by minute 70,
your motor cortex (the brain region that plans precise foot placement)
suppresses fine motor output to protect overall function, so the
signal to your foot arrives weaker and later. that is why your touch
lands 30-40 cm farther than it did in the first half.
```

```
shoebox-sized target you tape to the wall. the smaller target
forces your motor cortex to maintain precision, so your touch
stays clean in minute 80 when the defender expects you to lose
the ball because your brain stays sharp under late-game pressure.
```
