## Preset 1: Biological ROI Ranking

### Use Case

Use this when the post needs to rank habits, drills, routines, recovery tools,
or mental-performance actions by how much biological return they give the
athlete. The format works across sports when the desire is concrete and
position-agnostic: confidence that survives pressure, cleaner decisions, faster
reads, steadier execution, better recovery, or sharper late-game performance.

Do not use it for random trivia lists. Every ranked item must serve the same
athlete, the same sport context, and the same desired outcome.

### Core Viewer Promise

The athlete learns which behaviors produce the highest biological return for a
specific performance desire. The emotional pull is: "I train hard, but I want to
know which actions actually change the system that fails under pressure."

### Hook Behavior

Use this shape:

```text
5 [sport-specific habits/drills/routines] ranked by biological ROI:
([plain-language mechanism promise])
```

Approved examples:

- `5 pre-game habits ranked by biological ROI: (how to force your nervous system to perform on command)`
- `5 basketball warm-up habits ranked by biological ROI: (which ones keep your shot from tightening under pressure)`
- `5 tennis reset habits ranked by biological ROI: (how to stop one double fault from becoming three)`
- `5 combat-sport walkout habits ranked by biological ROI: (how to keep your nervous system from dumping your gas tank early)`

The hook must name the desired outcome or the failure state. Do not make the
hook about science alone.

### Flow Structure

Preferred structure mirrors verified_byhumans Post 2:

1. **Hero:** Numbered ranking + biological ROI phrase + parenthetical mechanism
   promise.
2. **Body slides:** Ranked items from lowest to highest ROI (`#5` to `#1`).
3. **Each body slide:**
   - title: the habit, drill, routine, or behavior
   - label: `the biological roi (why it works):`
   - top explanation: mechanism + why the old/default state fails
   - center image frame: evidence photo, sport cue, or mechanism visual
   - lower section: `the result:` + tangible sport outcome
   - source line: journal/source citation for strong claims

Default slide count is **6 slides**: hero + 5 ranked body slides. Add a **7th
summary stack slide** only when the post needs an explicit routine, tool stack,
or camouflaged product handoff. Do not add a generic closing slide.

### Optional 7th Slide: Protocol Stack

Use when the ranked items should become one usable routine.

Structure:

```text
the optimal routine (how they stack):
[build your sequence / routine title]

[concise sequence of actions in order]

the result: [regulated system + concrete sport outcome]

scientific source: [multi-modal routine / sports psychology source]
```

For product integration, w(inner) may appear only as one native tool inside the
routine, not as the point of the post. Approved wording direction:

- `use w(inner) to run position-specific visualization audios that match your exact role`
- `use w(inner) for personalized visualization audio around your sport, position, and specific moment`

Avoid product guarantees such as "w(inner) fixes confidence" or "w(inner)
guarantees clutch performance."

### Copy Rhythm

- Keep the repeated labels. They make the ranking feel clinical and easy to scan.
- The mechanism must earn the ranking. Do not rank by opinion.
- The result section must make the benefit physical or game-visible: first touch
  feels automatic, game speed feels slower, the shot stays loose, the reset
  happens before the next point, the athlete stops calculating and starts
  reacting.
- Use numbers, timing windows, sets, reps, or specific pre-game windows when they
  are available.
- Maintain line-fill discipline. The dense centered blocks should form visual
  rectangles, not ragged triangles.

### Cross-Sport Transfer Pattern

For each sport, define the same five inputs before drafting:

| Input | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Sport context | Which sport and pressure moment does this target? |
| Shared avatar | Which position-agnostic athlete can see themselves here? |
| Desire | What does the athlete want to feel or execute? |
| Failure state | What breaks under pressure? |
| Ranked system | Which 5 habits/drills/routines can be ranked by biological return? |

Example transfer directions:

- Soccer: pre-game confidence, first-touch calm, penalty pressure, game-speed reads.
- Basketball: free-throw routine, open-shot hesitation, late-game shooting touch.
- Tennis: serve yips, double-fault reset, break-point composure.
- Football: pre-snap reads, post-error reset, contact confidence.
- Combat sports: walkout arousal, first-contact response, gas-tank panic.

### Image Implications

- Hero: high-contrast B&W athlete portrait with clean negative space.
- Body: centered `800x450` evidence frame from `design.md`.
- Body image should clarify the habit or mechanism: pre-game playlist, film
  study, breathing, visualization, sprint burst, routine stack, sport-specific
  cue, or simple physiology visual.
- Avoid decorative stock photos. Each image should make the habit feel usable.

### Product Fit

Strongest fit: mental-performance posts where visualization, scenario rehearsal,
match-day prep, or pre-competition routines are already part of the protocol.

w(inner) may be named as a tool for personalized visualization audio by sport,
position, and situation. Keep it camouflaged inside the routine stack. Do not
turn the slide into an app ad, CTA, logo reveal, pricing mention, or download
prompt.

### Risks And Guardrails

- Ranking implies comparison. Either support the order with research or frame it
  as a practical ranking for the chosen routine.
- Avoid neuroscience overreach. Do not claim visualization, breathing, or sprints
  guarantee confidence.
- Do not use "biological ROI" as a decoration. The slide must explain the
  biology and translate it into a sport outcome.
- Do not let the list become multi-avatar. Every item should serve the same
  athlete desire.
- Do not add a generic close. If slide 7 exists, it must summarize the usable
  routine or product/tool stack.
