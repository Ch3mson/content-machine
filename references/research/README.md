# Research Intake Guide

Use this guide before creating `sources.md` for any new account or niche.

The goal is not to make every slideshow academic. The goal is to decide which claims need support, which claims can be framed as opinion or observation, and which topics should be avoided until approved.

## When To Use This

Use this guide when:

- Creating a new account folder.
- Entering a new niche.
- Changing the account from entertainment/opinion into advice, education, health, finance, legal, or product claims.
- Adding a new recurring content pillar.
- Making strong app claims tied to outcomes.

## Research Questions To Answer

Before writing the account `sources.md`, answer these for the niche:

1. What topics will this account talk about often?
2. Which topics can cause harm if wrong?
3. Which topics need external sources before being stated as fact?
4. Which topics can be treated as opinion, taste, personal experience, or commentary?
5. Which authorities matter in this niche?
6. Which sources are low-quality or should be avoided?
7. What claims would the audience distrust without proof?
8. What claims would platforms, regulators, or users consider sensitive?
9. What claims can be made only if the app brief or claim bank supports them?
10. What recurring terms need precise definitions?

## Source Types By Use

| Source type | Best for | Watchouts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary research | Scientific, medical, behavioral, or technical claims | Harder to interpret; do not overstate findings |
| Official guidance | Health, finance, legal, platform, or safety claims | May be broad and conservative |
| Expert explainers | Translating complex topics into usable rules | Check credentials and conflicts |
| Industry reports | Market behavior, trends, audience habits | Often biased toward the publisher |
| Product proof | App benefits, screenshots, demos, user outcomes | Must match the real product |
| Community examples | Voice, beliefs, objections, language | Not proof by itself |
| Competitor examples | Flow, content angles, framing patterns | Copy structure only |

## Claim Risk Levels

Use these labels in each account `sources.md`.

| Risk | Meaning | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Low | Taste, opinion, observation, or harmless practical advice | Source optional |
| Medium | Advice that could change user behavior | Source preferred or frame as experience |
| High | Health, money, legal, safety, or measurable outcome claim | Source required |
| Product | Claim about the app's ability, benefit, or result | Must come from `product/app-brief.md` or `product/claim-bank.md` |

## Account sources.md Setup Process

For each new account:

1. Define the niche and audience.
2. List 5-10 recurring content pillars.
3. Mark each pillar as low, medium, high, or product risk.
4. Identify source types needed for each pillar.
5. Add approved sources or mark research gaps.
6. Add draft claims that need verification.
7. Add words or claims to avoid.
8. Revisit the file after the first sample slideshow.

## Prompt

Use this when asking for a new niche research plan:

```text
Create a sources.md research plan for a new slideshow account.

Niche:
[niche]

Audience:
[audience]

Account goal:
[saves/shares, follows, comments, product awareness, etc.]

Product relevance:
[how the app may or may not connect]

Known content pillars:
[list any known topics]

Use references/research/README.md.

Return:
- recurring content pillars
- claim risk levels
- source types needed
- likely approved source categories
- claims that need approval
- claims or wording to avoid
- a draft account sources.md structure
```
