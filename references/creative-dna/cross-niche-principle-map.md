## 1) Failure warning

- Core move: warn the viewer about what breaks progress if ignored.
- Reusable effect: creates urgency, self-audit, and corrective attention.
- Best use: when the audience already suspects they are doing something wrong.
- Transfer cue: lead with a consequence, then show the fix.

## 2) Invisible competition

- Core move: frame improvement as a race happening off-screen.
- Reusable effect: turns ordinary behavior into status competition.
- Best use: when the viewer needs a reason to care about unseen effort.
- Transfer cue: compare private habits, not public results.

## 3) Self-sabotage diagnosis

- Core move: name the behavior pattern that quietly blocks progress.
- Reusable effect: gives the audience a diagnosis they can recognize instantly.
- Best use: when shame can be converted into clarity.
- Transfer cue: “you think it’s X, but it’s really Y.”

## 4) Red-flag / fix

- Core move: identify a warning sign and pair it with a corrective action.
- Reusable effect: easy to scan, easy to save, easy to apply.
- Best use: when the post needs practical utility without long explanation.
- Transfer cue: one red flag, one fix, one reason it matters.

## 5) Ranked-review

- Core move: sort options, mistakes, or priorities by importance.
- Reusable effect: reduces complexity and signals judgment.
- Best use: when the audience wants decision help.
- Transfer cue: top-to-bottom ranking with a clear winner.

## 6) Challenge standard

- Core move: set a tougher benchmark than the audience expects.
- Reusable effect: raises perceived seriousness and identity pressure.
- Best use: when the account wants to define the new normal.
- Transfer cue: “most people do X; the standard is Y.”

## 7) Surprising proof stat

- Core move: use a number that feels too large, too fast, or too specific to ignore.
- Reusable effect: makes the claim feel concrete enough to investigate.
- Best use: when a stat can anchor curiosity before explanation.
- Transfer cue: headline the number, then explain why it happened.

## 8) Private habit stack

- Core move: reveal compact rituals that happen away from the spotlight.
- Reusable effect: creates intimacy, discipline, and compounding logic.
- Best use: for performance, identity, and self-trust content.
- Transfer cue: habits + time window + quiet edge.

## 9) Hyperbolic transformation

- Core move: exaggerate the result enough to trigger disbelief.
- Reusable effect: opens a curiosity gap that a list or story can resolve.
- Best use: when the result is visual, fast, or socially impressive.
- Transfer cue: extreme outcome first, mechanism second.

## 10) ROI ranking

- Core move: rank actions by return on effort.
- Reusable effect: makes the audience feel efficient and strategic.
- Best use: when the post needs to simplify a big problem into priorities.
- Transfer cue: highest payoff first, lowest payoff last.

## How the families connect

- Failure warning and self-sabotage diagnosis both create corrective urgency.
- Invisible competition and private habit stack both reward unseen effort.
- Red-flag / fix and challenge standard both convert judgment into action.
- Ranked-review and ROI ranking both help the viewer decide what matters most.
- Surprising proof stat and hyperbolic transformation both use numerical shock to earn attention.

## Cross-target application logic

Across all 10 targets, the main transfer question is not “what niche?” but “what mechanism family fits the audience tension?”

- If the audience fears being behind, use failure warning or invisible competition.
- If the audience is confused, use self-sabotage diagnosis or red-flag / fix.
- If the audience needs prioritization, use ranked-review or ROI ranking.
- If the audience needs inspiration without fluff, use challenge standard or surprising proof stat.
- If the audience responds to hidden effort or dramatic change, use private habit stack or hyperbolic transformation.

## Shared adaptation rule

Keep the mechanism; change the proof surface.

- For athlete-max: translate every family into mechanism-first performance language.
- For athlete-stories: translate every family into fact-led pressure, origin, or visualization arcs.
