# Athlete Stories — Creative DNA Account Profile

## Account Creative DNA Summary

- Account niche: athlete origin stories and mental visualization.
- Core frame: legendary athletes are built mentally before the arena sees the result.
- Winning posts are fact-led pressure arcs: origin, struggle, obsession, mental method, public proof, native product bridge, quote close.
- Best content feels blunt, grounded, aspirational, and specific — never generic motivation.

## Audience Tensions

- Aspiring athletes want proof that greatness came from struggle and preparation, not vague talent.
- Sports fans want a specific hidden fact that reframes a famous athlete.
- Young competitors struggle with pressure, self-doubt, and the gap between physical training and mental readiness.
- The account wins when it reveals a mental habit, private standard, or visualization routine that made public performance feel inevitable.

## Approved Voice/Design/Image Constraints

- Voice: editorial narrator, blunt and athlete-specific. First person only for the product bridge.
- Rhythm: compact fact density with a slippery-slope arc where each slide makes the next feel necessary.
- Claims: use numbers, routines, places, opponents, coaches, records, and sourced quotes before adjectives.
- Product bridge: late-story, brief, first-person, native to the athlete's mental method. Never a sales slide.
- Design: locked 7-slide `1080x1920` full-bleed B&W story system.
- Typography: centered white Arial Bold with black outline; no color highlights or decorative boxes.
- Images: full-canvas athlete photos, B&W, vintage paper overlay, readable face/action after crop.

## Existing Proven Patterns

- **LeBron preset:** origin hardship → work/study → visualization → public transformation → quiet w(inner) mirror → identity quote.
- **Michael Jordan preset:** second-person challenge → humiliation/rivalry → body not enough → visualization as edge → product echo → belief quote.
- **Legendperform influence:** compact fact/stat/quote carousels.
- **Asdicipline influence:** pressure hooks and discipline standards, but must be made less motivational and more fact-led.

## Best-Fit Mechanism Families from Creative DNA

- **failure-warning** — strong fit for athlete almost loses edge, wastes talent, or ignores basics.
- **invisible-competition** — strong fit for rival pressure, unseen work, and private preparation.
- **self-sabotage-diagnosis** — medium fit when told through a factual athlete arc, not advice content.
- **red-flag-fix** — medium fit if the red flag becomes a visible pressure signal in a story.
- **ranked-review** — medium fit for ranking athlete methods, but must become story logic rather than utility content.
- **challenge-standard** — strong fit for elite hidden standards and “could you survive this?” arcs.
- **surprising-proof-stat** — strongest fit for opening athlete stories with a number that demands explanation.
- **private-habit-stack** — strong fit for unseen mental routines before public breakout.
- **hyperbolic-transformation** — medium fit only when grounded in real transformation proof, not fake time promises.
- **roi-ranking** — weaker as a direct format, but useful for “ranked mental habits that built a legend.”

## Weak/Risky Mechanism Fits

- Pure how-to carousels can violate the story-first promise.
- Science explanations can feel too clinical unless they serve an athlete arc.
- Hyperbolic transformation can feel fake if not backed by biography or visible career change.
- Red-flag/fix content can become generic advice unless tied to a named athlete moment.
- Product mention cannot appear before the mental method is earned by the story.
