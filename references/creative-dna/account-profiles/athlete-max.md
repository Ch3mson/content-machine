# Athlete Max — Creative DNA Account Profile

## Account Creative DNA Summary
- Competitive athletes want a real edge, not hype.
- Core frame: **clinical coach who reads journals so you don't have to**.
- Winning posts turn a private performance problem into a mechanism, then into a game-visible fix.
- Best content feels science-backed, practical, dense, and calm.

## Audience Tensions
- Training hard but not knowing what actually transfers to performance.
- Nerves, pressure, fatigue, and late-game drop-off that feel like “mental” failure.
- Wanting clarity without bro-science, motivational fluff, or jargon.
- Wanting a save-worthy edge they can use today.

## Approved Voice/Design/Image Constraints
- Voice: direct, mechanism-first, plain language, never motivational.
- Use accessible analogies; avoid locker-room-unfriendly jargon.
- Structure: hook → problem → mechanism → fix/result → source.
- Design: white background, black text, sparing deep red accents, 1080x1350 baseline.
- Body slides use fixed centered evidence frames; no text over imagery.
- Images: clean, clinical, high-contrast, desaturated/B&W, performance-specific.
- Product mention: extremely camouflaged; never app-ad-like.

## Existing Proven Patterns
- Problem-first mechanism posts that name the failure before the science.
- “What to avoid” → “the fix” slides with simple performance translation.
- Biological ROI ranking as a reusable stack for habits/routines.
- Evidence-photo body slides with source lines for strong claims.
- Clean, clinical framing like `the biological roi (why it works):`.

## Best-Fit Mechanism Families from Creative DNA
- **failure-warning** — strong fit for late-game drop-off, hesitation, and pressure mistakes.
- **invisible-competition** — strong fit when the athlete is fighting fatigue, attention, or nervous system load.
- **self-sabotage-diagnosis** — strong fit for hidden habits that quietly wreck performance.
- **red-flag-fix** — strong fit for “stop doing this” recovery, prep, and game-day errors.
- **ranked-review** — strongest fit; the account already validates ranking logic.
- **challenge-standard** — strong fit for replacing vague effort with specific performance standards.
- **surprising-proof-stat** — good when sourced carefully and tied to a real outcome.
- **private-habit-stack** — strong fit for pre-game routines and camouflaged protocol content.
- **hyperbolic-transformation** — weaker; risks sounding motivational or fake.
- **roi-ranking** — strongest fit for this account.

## Weak/Risky Mechanism Fits
- Hyperbolic transformation: too hype-coded unless tightly grounded.
- Broad identity content: can drift into generic athlete inspiration.
- Pure stat posts: risk feeling dry unless the stat answers a performance pain.
- Product-first stacks: risk breaking the invisible integration rule.
- Jargon-led science explainers: risk losing save/share clarity.
