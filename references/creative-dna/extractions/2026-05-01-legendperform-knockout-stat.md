## Surface Topic

## Reusable Principle

The deeper creative mechanism that could transfer to another niche.

Write this as an abstract pattern, not as source-specific wording.

- Principle name: Extreme proof stack with explanation
- Principle summary: Start with a shocking stat, then stack multiple supporting facts so the number feels earned rather than inflated. The post turns curiosity into belief by showing the mechanisms behind the anomaly.
- Safe rewrite formula: A rare stat looks unbelievable until you see the layered reasons it happens.

## Psychological Mechanism

What makes the viewer care, pause, believe, or keep swiping?

- Mechanism: Surprise followed by mechanism confirmation
- Why it works: The audience first reacts to the outlier, then stays for the explanation that makes the outlier feel inevitable.
- Viewer belief being challenged: Big numbers are usually exaggeration or hype.
- Viewer desire or insecurity being activated: Desire to understand what creates elite efficiency and to borrow the logic behind it.

## Hook Archetype

- Archetype: extreme-proof-stack
- First-frame promise: This stat is so unusual that it needs an explanation.
- Hook engine: ranking
- What must not be copied verbatim: The exact statistic wording and the specific fighter/weight-class claim without verification.

## Emotional Lever

Select and explain the dominant levers.

- Fear: Low; this is more awe than threat.
- Status: High; elite performance is framed as rare and prestigious.
- Shame: Low; the tone is admiring.
- Curiosity: Very high; the stat demands an explanation.
- Aspiration: High; viewers want to understand the formula behind dominance.
- Identity: Moderate; hard-core sports fans identify with seeing through hype.
- Urgency: Low; the post is not time-pressured.
- Relief: Moderate; the explanation resolves doubt.
- Other: Awe / disbelief.

## Curiosity Gap

What question does the viewer need answered?

- Open loop: How can someone produce that many knockouts in a division built to minimize them?
- Why the viewer keeps swiping/watching: The post keeps adding evidence that the stat is real and explainable.
- Payoff promised: A layered explanation that turns the stat from shock into pattern.

## Proof Pattern

What makes the claim feel believable?

- Proof type: stats
- Specific proof moments: knockout percentage, comparison to weight-class norms, punch power, speed/timing, undefeated / title-level success, quote support.
- Claim-risk notes: Strongest when the stat is sourced and the comparison class is clear; avoid cherry-picked or unverifiable records.
- What would need verification before using this in our account: Exact win rate, division comparisons, and fighter record details.

## Transformation Arc

How does the post move the viewer from current state to desired state?

```text
Before state:
Viewer sees a headline stat and assumes it is hype.
Trigger / problem:
The stat conflicts with normal expectations for the sport.
Hidden mechanism:
The fighter wins through speed, timing, and technical efficiency.
New belief:
Outlier results usually have a concrete performance reason.
Action or standard:
Look for the mechanism behind rare outcomes, not just the headline.
After state:
Viewer understands the result as the product of a repeatable edge.
```

- Body-slide layout pattern: One image per slide, text centered, minimal clutter, strong contrast.
- Text hierarchy: One headline stat, then a single explanatory sentence or quote.
