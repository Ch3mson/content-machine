## Connection

Every reference account injects implicit or explicit value into every slide — even problem slides, even mechanism slides, even warning slides. The viewer never swipes to a "just explaining" slide. Seven distinct value-injection patterns were identified across chasingpeaks0, verified_byhumans, kingdubar, and tgaskins3. Applied to athlete-max's loss-aversion angle, these patterns converted a flat 5-stage problem cascade into a balanced 10-slide post where every slide earns its keep: problem slides deliver relief and self-recognition; fix slides deliver mechanism-backed protocols and training systems.

## Transferable Principle

**Every slide in a carousel must inject one immediate value type.** A slide that only names a problem or explains a mechanism without an immediate reader benefit will lose the swipe. The seven value types are:

1. **Diagnostic relief** — "You're not X. Your body is doing Y. Now you can fix it."
2. **Self-recognition** — Mirror a private frustration. "That happens to me."
3. **Mechanism clarity** — Explain WHY it's happening. Understanding = value.
4. **Performance cost translation** — Quantify the in-game damage. Specific numbers.
5. **Competitive urgency** — Name what's being lost right now. Wake-up call.
6. **Expensive lesson free** — Someone else paid the cost so you don't have to.
7. **Immediate fix** — Diagnosis + correction paired in the same slide. No waiting.

## Why It Matters

Generic athlete-max drafts treat problem slides as setup and fix slides as payoff. This produces front-heavy posts where curiosity dies before the solution arrives. The value-injection rule forces every slide to earn retention independently, which means:
- Problem slides must include self-recognition, diagnostic relief, or cost quantification
- Fix slides must include mechanism explanation (why the fix works), not just protocol
- The problem-to-fix ratio needs at least 40% fix slides for conviction
- Hook scoring requires persona passes (Bored Athlete / Account-Native Scroller / Athlete Skeptic)
