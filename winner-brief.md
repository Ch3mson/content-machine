# w(inner) — Product Brief

## What it is

w(inner) is a mobile mental performance app for elite and aspiring athletes. It generates personalized visualization audio tracks on demand using an AI diagnostic chat, then renders them as spoken narration the athlete listens to before training, before sleep, or before they walk out for competition.

It is not a meditation app. It is not a chatbot. It is a structured psychological training system — three pillars, nine tracks, custom audio every time.

---

## Value proposition

**"Play like you know you can."**

Most athletes have the physical ability. What stops them is mental — hesitation, self-doubt, overthinking, stress, the voice in their head on the biggest moments. w(inner) closes the gap between the athlete they are and the athlete they know they could be by giving them a personal mental training system that fits their sport, their position, and their specific struggles.

The promise is simple: tell us what you're working on, and we'll generate a custom audio routine — built for you, voiced for your situation, designed to rewire how you show up.

---

## Who it is for

- Youth and university-level athletes (primary)
- Semi-pro and elite competitors looking for an edge
- Athletes with real ability who underperform mentally
- Athletes returning from injury or a confidence dip
- Athletes preparing for a high-stakes match, trial, or tournament

The core user is the athlete who says: *"I know I can play better than I do. My mind gets in the way."*

---

## The user benefits

1. **Build unshakeable game-day confidence** — pre-match programming that locks the athlete into the version of themselves that performs.
2. **Silence the voice that holds you back** — handle self-doubt, anxiety, and overthinking without clinical or therapeutic framing.
3. **Become the athlete you were meant to be** — aspirational identity work, not just symptom management.
4. **Get mental reps when the body can't** — visualize technique and tactics during recovery, off-days, or injury.
5. **Track mental performance over time** — six measurable mental attributes (Confidence, Focus, Vision, Resilience, Composure, Drive) on a living radar chart that updates from honest self-assessment.
6. **Personal, not generic** — every audio is built around the user's sport, position, opponents, venues, and triggers.

---

## What the user can do

### 1. Build a personal Athlete Identity
Onboarding captures name, gender, sport, position, and what the user honestly struggles with. The system computes a starting mental baseline across six attributes and shows it as a radar chart.

### 2. Choose a pillar based on what they need today
- **Daily Visualization** — morning identity programming (~5 min)
- **Scenario-Based** — mental reps, technique drilling, error correction during the training week (3–5 min)
- **Match Day Prep** — pre-competition state management, night-before anchor, or game-day activation (3–10 min)

### 3. Run an AI diagnostic chat
Pre-written, structured questions extract the specific variables the audio will be built from — the opponent's energy, the worst-case scenario, the physical trigger, the moment of victory. Four questions, every time.

### 4. Receive a custom audio track
The system generates a 3–10 minute spoken visualization, voiced by xAI TTS, written for the athlete's exact situation. The audio is theirs to keep and replay.

### 5. See the projected mental impact
Before listening, a Track Summary screen shows which attributes the track is designed to move and the projected change on the radar chart.

### 6. Check in honestly after a session
Optional 1–2 question reflection. Real attributes only move when the athlete reports real-world experience — the radar stays an honest mirror, not a vanity meter.

## Convincing angles (sport-agnostic)

These are the universal desires the product can be sold against. Any sport-specific niche layer can be built on top of these.

### Performance angles
1. **The talent–performance gap** — "You have the skill. You just can't access it when it matters." The athlete who underperforms relative to ability.
2. **Game-day confidence** — Show up to competition feeling certain instead of nervous.
3. **Clutch performance** — Be the athlete who wants the ball in the last minute, not the one hiding from it.
4. **Mental reps** — Get better without your body. Train technique while injured, recovering, traveling, or resting.
5. **Slump breaker** — Overwrite a bad pattern (missed shots, errors, freezes) before it becomes identity.

### Emotional / psychological angles
6. **Silencing self-doubt** — The voice that says "you're not good enough" before big moments.
7. **Pre-competition anxiety** — Sleep the night before, eat the morning of, walk into the venue calm.
8. **Recovery from failure** — Bounce back from a bad game, missed selection, or public mistake.
9. **Imposter syndrome at a higher level** — Moving up a division, joining a stronger team, playing against names.
10. **The fear of letting people down** — Parents, coaches, teammates, scouts, self.

### Identity / aspiration angles
11. **Becoming the version of yourself that wins** — Identity-level visualization, not symptom management.
12. **Modeling a hero** — Channel a specific legendary athlete's traits and presence.
13. **Legacy and long-term vision** — Reconnect daily training to the ultimate goal (pro contract, scholarship, championship, Olympic team).
14. **Leadership identity** — Captains, leaders, vocal players who need to be the calm in chaos.

### Edge / competitive angles
15. **The 1% edge** — Elite athletes already do the physical work; mental is the remaining lever.
16. **Pre-trial / pre-scout / pre-trial-game preparation** — High-stakes one-shot moments where you only get one chance.
17. **Returning from injury** — Rebuild confidence in the body and the timing of decisions.
18. **Sport transition** — New position, new system, new coach, new level.

### Habit / lifestyle angles
19. **Morning operating system** — A 5-minute daily practice that sets the tone for everything else.
20. **Night-before sleep aid** — A track that ends in victory feeling and drifts into deep sleep.
21. **Walkout ritual** — The 3-minute final activation before stepping into competition.
22. **Travel-friendly mental gym** — Works in a hotel, on a bus, in a locker room — no equipment, no signal needed once downloaded.

### Trust / credibility angles
23. **Honest progress, not gamified streaks** — Attributes only move when the athlete reports real-world change.
24. **Built around YOUR sport, position, and triggers** — Not a generic meditation library.
25. **Coach, performance manager, technical analyst — voiced as the role you need** — Daily uses a Mindset Coach, Scenario uses a Technical Coach, Match Day uses a Performance Manager.

## How the layering works

The base layers are universal: confidence, focus, anxiety, identity, mental reps, recovery, clutch, sleep, activation. The sport-specific layer comes from:

- **Sport selection** at onboarding (filters supported list)
- **Position** (where applicable) refining the language and scenarios
- **Struggle pills** curated per sport + position (e.g., a soccer winger sees "Lacking confidence in 1v1 duels," a tennis player sees "Double-fault spirals")
- **Diagnostic answers** the user gives in chat — opponent, venue, trigger, scenario — make the audio bespoke

This means a marketing or onboarding angle like "Silence the voice that holds you back" stays universal, but the *experience* the athlete gets is hyper-specific to their context.
